## Problem
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{x^2\sqrt{x^2 - 25}} \, dx$.

## Rule Used
**Trigonometric substitution (7.4):** for $\sqrt{x^2 - a^2}$ with $a = 5$, use $x = 5\sec\theta$.

## Solution
**Step 1 — Substitute.** Let $x = 5\sec\theta$, $dx = 5\sec\theta\tan\theta \, d\theta$. Then
$$\sqrt{x^2 - 25} = \sqrt{25\sec^2\theta - 25} = 5\tan\theta$$

**Step 2 — Rewrite the integral.**
$$\int \frac{5\sec\theta\tan\theta}{(25\sec^2\theta)(5\tan\theta)} \, d\theta = \frac{1}{25}\int \frac{1}{\sec\theta} \, d\theta = \frac{1}{25}\int \cos\theta \, d\theta$$

**Step 3 — Integrate.**
$$= \frac{1}{25}\sin\theta + C$$

**Step 4 — Return to $x$** (hypotenuse $x$, adjacent $5$, opposite $\sqrt{x^2 - 25}$):
$$\sin\theta = \frac{\sqrt{x^2 - 25}}{x}$$

## Answer
$$\int \frac{1}{x^2\sqrt{x^2 - 25}} \, dx = \frac{\sqrt{x^2 - 25}}{25x} + C$$

## Check by Differentiation
$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{\sqrt{x^2-25}}{25x}\right] = \frac{\frac{x^2}{\sqrt{x^2-25}} - \sqrt{x^2-25}}{25x^2} = \frac{x^2 - (x^2-25)}{25x^2\sqrt{x^2-25}} = \frac{1}{x^2\sqrt{x^2-25}} \checkmark$$
