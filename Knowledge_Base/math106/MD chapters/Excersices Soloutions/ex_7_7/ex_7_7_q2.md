## Problem
Determine whether $\displaystyle\int_{-\infty}^{0} \frac{1}{(x - 1)^3} \, dx$ converges or diverges; if it converges, find its value.

## Rule Used
**Definition of the improper integral with infinite lower limit:**
$$\int_{-\infty}^{0} f(x)\,dx = \lim_{t \to -\infty}\int_t^0 f(x)\,dx$$

## Solution
**Step 1 — Integrate.**
$$\int (x - 1)^{-3} \, dx = \frac{(x - 1)^{-2}}{-2} = -\frac{1}{2(x - 1)^2}$$

**Step 2 — Apply limits.**
$$\lim_{t \to -\infty}\left[-\frac{1}{2(x - 1)^2}\right]_t^0 = \lim_{t \to -\infty}\left(-\frac{1}{2(0-1)^2} + \frac{1}{2(t - 1)^2}\right) = -\frac{1}{2} + 0$$

## Answer
The integral **converges** and
$$\int_{-\infty}^{0} \frac{1}{(x - 1)^3} \, dx = -\frac{1}{2}$$
