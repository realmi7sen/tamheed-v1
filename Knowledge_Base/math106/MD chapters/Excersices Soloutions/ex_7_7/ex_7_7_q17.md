## Problem
Determine whether $\displaystyle\int_1^{\infty} \frac{\ln x}{x} \, dx$ converges or diverges; if it converges, find its value.

## Rule Used
**Definition of the improper integral** + the substitution $u = \ln x$.

## Solution
**Step 1 — Antiderivative.** With $u = \ln x$, $du = \frac{1}{x}dx$:
$$\int \frac{\ln x}{x} \, dx = \int u \, du = \frac{(\ln x)^2}{2}$$

**Step 2 — Apply the limit.**
$$\lim_{t \to \infty}\left[\frac{(\ln x)^2}{2}\right]_1^t = \lim_{t \to \infty}\frac{(\ln t)^2}{2} - 0 = \infty$$

## Answer
The limit grows without bound, so the integral **diverges**.
