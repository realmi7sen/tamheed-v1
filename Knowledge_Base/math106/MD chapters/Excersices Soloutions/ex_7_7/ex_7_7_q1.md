## Problem
Determine whether $\displaystyle\int_1^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^{4/3}} \, dx$ converges or diverges; if it converges, find its value.

## Rule Used
**Definition of the improper integral with infinite upper limit:**
$$\int_1^{\infty} f(x)\,dx = \lim_{t \to \infty}\int_1^t f(x)\,dx$$

## Solution
**Step 1 — Replace the infinite limit and integrate.**
$$\lim_{t \to \infty}\int_1^t x^{-4/3} \, dx = \lim_{t \to \infty}\Big[-3x^{-1/3}\Big]_1^t = \lim_{t \to \infty}\left(-\frac{3}{t^{1/3}} + 3\right)$$

**Step 2 — Evaluate the limit.** As $t \to \infty$, $\dfrac{3}{t^{1/3}} \to 0$.

## Answer
The integral **converges** and
$$\int_1^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^{4/3}} \, dx = 3$$
