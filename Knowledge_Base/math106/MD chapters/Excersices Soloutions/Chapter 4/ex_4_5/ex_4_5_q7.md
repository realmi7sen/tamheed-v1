## Problem

It will follow from the results in Section 4.6 that
$$\int_1^4 x^2 \, dx = 21 \quad \text{and} \quad \int_1^4 x \, dx = \frac{15}{2}.$$
Use these facts to evaluate the integral.
$$\int_1^4 (3x^2 + 5) \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Linearity Properties of the Definite Integral**
$$\int_a^b [cf(x) + g(x)] \, dx = c\int_a^b f(x) \, dx + \int_a^b g(x) \, dx$$
$$\int_a^b c \, dx = c(b - a)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Apply linearity properties to separate the integral**
We can break the integral into two parts and factor out the constant multiplier:
$$\int_1^4 (3x^2 + 5) \, dx = 3\int_1^4 x^2 \, dx + \int_1^4 5 \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Substitute the known values**
We are given that $\int_1^4 x^2 \, dx = 21$. For the constant integral, we use the property $\int_a^b c \, dx = c(b - a)$:
$$= 3(21) + 5(4 - 1)$$

**Step 3 — Simplify the expression**
$$= 63 + 5(3)$$
$$= 63 + 15 = 78$$

---

## Answer

$$78$$
