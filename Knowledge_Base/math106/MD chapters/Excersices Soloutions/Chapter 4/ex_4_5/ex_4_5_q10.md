## Problem

It will follow from the results in Section 4.6 that
$$\int_1^4 x^2 \, dx = 21 \quad \text{and} \quad \int_1^4 x \, dx = \frac{15}{2}.$$
Use these facts to evaluate the integral.
$$\int_1^4 (3x + 2)^2 \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Linearity Properties of the Definite Integral**
$$\int_a^b [cf(x) + dg(x) + h(x)] \, dx = c\int_a^b f(x) \, dx + d\int_a^b g(x) \, dx + \int_a^b h(x) \, dx$$
$$\int_a^b c \, dx = c(b - a)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Expand the integrand**
Before applying integral properties, expand the binomial $(3x + 2)^2 = 9x^2 + 12x + 4$:
$$\int_1^4 (9x^2 + 12x + 4) \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Apply linearity properties to separate the terms**
Split the integral into three separate integrals and factor out the constants:
$$= 9\int_1^4 x^2 \, dx + 12\int_1^4 x \, dx + \int_1^4 4 \, dx$$

**Step 3 — Substitute the known values**
Substitute the given values for the integrals of $x^2$ and $x$, and evaluate the constant integral:
$$= 9(21) + 12\left(\frac{15}{2}\right) + 4(4 - 1)$$

**Step 4 — Simplify the arithmetic**
$$= 189 + 6(15) + 4(3)$$
$$= 189 + 90 + 12 = 291$$

---

## Answer

$$291$$
