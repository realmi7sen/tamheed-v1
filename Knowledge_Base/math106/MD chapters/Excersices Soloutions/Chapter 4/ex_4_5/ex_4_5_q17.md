## Problem

Express as one integral.
$$\int_5^1 f(x) \, dx + \int_{-3}^5 f(x) \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Additive Interval Property**
For any numbers $a, b,$ and $c$, assuming the integrals exist:
$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx + \int_b^c f(x) \, dx = \int_a^c f(x) \, dx$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rearrange the sum**
Addition is commutative, so we can reorder the two integrals to clearly see the overlapping bound:
$$\int_{-3}^5 f(x) \, dx + \int_5^1 f(x) \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Apply the additive interval property**
Notice that the upper limit of the first integral ($5$) perfectly matches the lower limit of the second integral ($5$). We can combine them into a single integral starting from the lower limit of the first ($-3$) to the upper limit of the second ($1$):
$$= \int_{-3}^1 f(x) \, dx$$

---

## Answer

$$\int_{-3}^1 f(x) \, dx$$
