# Chapter 4 Review — 4.7 Numerical integration

> Problem bank. Each item keeps its original Chapter 4 Review Exercise number.
> Technique: Approximating definite integrals using the Midpoint, Trapezoidal, and Simpson's rules.

**Exer. 55–58: Approximate the definite integral using the indicated rule for the stated values of $n$.**

55. $\displaystyle\int_{0}^{2} \sin(x^2) \, dx$; \quad midpoint rule for $n = 5$ and $10$

56. $\displaystyle\int_{0}^{1} \cos\sqrt{x} \, dx$; \quad trapezoidal rule for $n = 10$ and $20$

57. $\displaystyle\int_{2}^{4} \sqrt{x^3 + x} \, dx$; \quad Simpson's rule for $n = 4$ and $8$

58. $\displaystyle\int_{0}^{5} \frac{1}{x + 2} \, dx$; \quad Simpson's rule for $n = 5$ and $20$
