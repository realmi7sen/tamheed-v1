## Problem

Evaluate the definite integral by regarding it as the area under the graph of a function.
$$\int_0^3 |x - 1| \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Integral Additivity**
$$\int_a^c f(x) \, dx = \int_a^b f(x) \, dx + \int_b^c f(x) \, dx$$

**Area of a Triangle**
$$A = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \text{base} \cdot \text{height}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Analyze the graph of the function**
The function $y = |x - 1|$ forms a "V" shape with its vertex where the inside of the absolute value is zero:
$$x - 1 = 0 \implies x = 1$$
Over the interval $[0, 3]$, the graph consists of two straight line segments, creating two right triangles above the $x$-axis that meet at the vertex $x = 1$.

**Step 2 — Find the area of the first triangle (from $x = 0$ to $x = 1$)**
- The base is $b_1 = 1 - 0 = 1$.
- The height is $h_1 = f(0) = |0 - 1| = 1$.
- The area is $A_1 = \frac{1}{2}(1)(1) = \frac{1}{2}$.

**Step 3 — Find the area of the second triangle (from $x = 1$ to $x = 3$)**
- The base is $b_2 = 3 - 1 = 2$.
- The height is $h_2 = f(3) = |3 - 1| = 2$.
- The area is $A_2 = \frac{1}{2}(2)(2) = 2$.

**Step 4 — Sum the areas**
The total integral is the sum of the areas of these two triangles:
$$\int_0^3 |x - 1| \, dx = A_1 + A_2 = \frac{1}{2} + 2 = \frac{5}{2}$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{5}{2}$$
