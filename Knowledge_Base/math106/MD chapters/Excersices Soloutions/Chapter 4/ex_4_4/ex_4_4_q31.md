## Problem

Evaluate the definite integral by regarding it as the area under the graph of a function.
$$\int_{-3}^2 (2x + 6) \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Area of a Triangle**
$$A = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \text{base} \cdot \text{height}$$

**Definite Integral as Area**
If $f(x) \geq 0$ on $[a, b]$, then $\int_a^b f(x) \, dx$ is the area under the graph of $f$ over $[a, b]$.

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Graph the function and identify the shape**
The function is $y = 2x + 6$, which represents a straight line.
Find the $x$-intercept by setting $y = 0$:
$$0 = 2x + 6 \implies x = -3$$
The region bounded by this line, the $x$-axis, and the vertical line $x = 2$ forms a right triangle because the lower limit of integration is exactly the $x$-intercept. The entire region lies above the $x$-axis.

**Step 2 — Determine the base and height of the triangle**
- The base $b$ of the triangle extends along the $x$-axis from $x = -3$ to $x = 2$:
  $$b = 2 - (-3) = 5$$
- The height $h$ is the value of the function at the right endpoint, $x = 2$:
  $$h = f(2) = 2(2) + 6 = 4 + 6 = 10$$

**Step 3 — Calculate the area**
Using the formula for the area of a triangle:
$$A = \frac{1}{2}bh = \frac{1}{2}(5)(10) = 25$$

---

## Answer

$$25$$
