## Problem

The given numbers determine a partition $P$ of an interval. (a) Find the length of each subinterval of $P$. (b) Find the norm $\|P\|$ of the partition.
$$P = \{0, 1.1, 2.6, 3.7, 4.1, 5\}$$

---

## Rule Used

**Subinterval Length and Norm of a Partition**
The length of the $k$-th subinterval $[x_{k-1}, x_k]$ is $\Delta x_k = x_k - x_{k-1}$.
The norm of the partition, denoted by $\|P\|$, is the length of the longest subinterval.

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Find the length of each subinterval ($\Delta x_k$)**
We calculate the difference between consecutive points in the partition $P$.
- $\Delta x_1 = 1.1 - 0 = 1.1$
- $\Delta x_2 = 2.6 - 1.1 = 1.5$
- $\Delta x_3 = 3.7 - 2.6 = 1.1$
- $\Delta x_4 = 4.1 - 3.7 = 0.4$
- $\Delta x_5 = 5 - 4.1 = 0.9$

**Step 2 — Find the norm $\|P\|$**
The norm $\|P\|$ is the maximum of the subinterval lengths calculated in Step 1.
$$\|P\| = \max(1.1, 1.5, 1.1, 0.4, 0.9) = 1.5$$

---

## Answer

(a) The lengths of the subintervals are $1.1, 1.5, 1.1, 0.4$, and $0.9$.
(b) $\|P\| = 1.5$
