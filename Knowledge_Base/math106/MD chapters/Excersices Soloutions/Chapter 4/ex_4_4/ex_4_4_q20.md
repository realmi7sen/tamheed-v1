## Problem

Given $\int_1^4 \sqrt{x} \, dx = \frac{14}{3}$, evaluate the integral.
$$\int_1^4 \sqrt{s} \, ds$$

---

## Rule Used

**Dummy Variable Property**
The value of a definite integral is independent of the variable chosen for the integration. The variable is merely a "dummy variable".
$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = \int_a^b f(t) \, dt = \int_a^b f(s) \, ds$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Recognize the identical integrand**
The function being integrated is the square root function over the interval $[1, 4]$. The only difference between the given integral and the requested integral is the letter used to represent the variable of integration ($x$ vs $s$).

**Step 2 — Apply the dummy variable property**
Because changing the variable letter does not alter the area under the curve, the two integrals are exactly equal:
$$\int_1^4 \sqrt{s} \, ds = \int_1^4 \sqrt{x} \, dx = \frac{14}{3}$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{14}{3}$$
