## Problem

Use Definition (4.16) to express each limit as a definite integral on the given interval $[a, b]$.
$$\lim_{\|P\| \to 0} \sum_{k=1}^n (3w_k^2 - 2w_k + 5) \Delta x_k; \quad [-1, 2]$$

---

## Rule Used

**Definition of the Definite Integral (4.16)**
$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = \lim_{\|P\| \to 0} \sum_{k=1}^n f(w_k) \Delta x_k$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify the function $f(x)$**
By comparing the given limit to the definition, we can see that the expression being evaluated at the sample point $w_k$ is the function $f$.
We replace $w_k$ with the continuous variable $x$:
$$f(x) = 3x^2 - 2x + 5$$

**Step 2 — Identify the limits of integration**
The problem specifies the interval $[a, b]$ as $[-1, 2]$.
Therefore, the lower limit of integration is $a = -1$ and the upper limit is $b = 2$.

**Step 3 — Construct the definite integral**
Substitute $f(x)$, $a$, and $b$ into the definite integral notation:
$$\int_{-1}^2 (3x^2 - 2x + 5) \, dx$$

---

## Answer

$$\int_{-1}^2 (3x^2 - 2x + 5) \, dx$$
