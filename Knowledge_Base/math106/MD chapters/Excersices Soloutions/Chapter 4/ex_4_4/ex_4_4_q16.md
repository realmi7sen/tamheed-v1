## Problem

Use Definition (4.16) to express each limit as a definite integral on the given interval $[a, b]$.
$$\lim_{\|P\| \to 0} \sum_{k=1}^n \pi(w_k^2 - 4) \Delta x_k; \quad [2, 3]$$

---

## Rule Used

**Definition of the Definite Integral (4.16)**
$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = \lim_{\|P\| \to 0} \sum_{k=1}^n f(w_k) \Delta x_k$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify the function $f(x)$**
By comparing the given limit of the Riemann sum to the definition, we find the function $f$ by replacing the sample point $w_k$ with the continuous variable $x$:
$$f(x) = \pi(x^2 - 4)$$

**Step 2 — Identify the limits of integration**
The problem explicitly provides the interval $[a, b]$ as $[2, 3]$.
Thus, the lower limit is $a = 2$ and the upper limit is $b = 3$.

**Step 3 — Construct the definite integral**
Substitute the function and limits into the definite integral notation:
$$\int_2^3 \pi(x^2 - 4) \, dx$$

---

## Answer

$$\int_2^3 \pi(x^2 - 4) \, dx$$
