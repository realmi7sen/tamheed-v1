## Problem

Evaluate the integral:
$$\int \left( \frac{1}{z^3} - \frac{3}{z^2} \right) \, dz$$

---

## Rule Used

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite the integrand with negative exponents**
To apply the power rule, we must express the fractions as powers of $z$:
$$\int \left( z^{-3} - 3z^{-2} \right) \, dz$$

**Step 2 — Separate the integral and apply the Power Rule**
Integrate each term by adding 1 to the exponent and dividing by the new exponent:
$$= \int z^{-3} \, dz - 3 \int z^{-2} \, dz$$
$$= \frac{z^{-2}}{-2} - 3 \left( \frac{z^{-1}}{-1} \right) + C$$

**Step 3 — Simplify and return to fractional form**
$$= -\frac{1}{2}z^{-2} + 3z^{-1} + C$$
$$= -\frac{1}{2z^2} + \frac{3}{z} + C$$

---

## Answer

$$-\frac{1}{2z^2} + \frac{3}{z} + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dz}\left[-\frac{1}{2}z^{-2} + 3z^{-1} + C\right] = -\frac{1}{2}(-2z^{-3}) + 3(-1z^{-2}) + 0 = z^{-3} - 3z^{-2} = \frac{1}{z^3} - \frac{3}{z^2} \quad \checkmark$$
