## Problem

Evaluate the integral:
$$\int (3x - 1)^2 \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Expand the integrand**
Since the general chain rule (substitution) is not introduced yet, we must expand the binomial square $(a-b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2$:
$$\int (9x^2 - 6x + 1) \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Apply the Power Rule to each term**
Integrate term by term:
$$= 9 \left( \frac{x^3}{3} \right) - 6 \left( \frac{x^2}{2} \right) + 1x + C$$

**Step 3 — Simplify the coefficients**
$$= 3x^3 - 3x^2 + x + C$$

---

## Answer

$$3x^3 - 3x^2 + x + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}[3x^3 - 3x^2 + x + C] = 9x^2 - 6x + 1$$
Factoring this quadratic gives back the original integrand:
$$= (3x - 1)^2 \quad \checkmark$$
