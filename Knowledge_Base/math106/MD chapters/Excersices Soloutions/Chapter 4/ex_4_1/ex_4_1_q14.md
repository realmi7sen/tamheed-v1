## Problem

Evaluate the integral:
$$\int (2x - 5)(3x + 1) \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Expand the integrand**
Multiply the binomials together (FOIL) before integrating:
$$\int (6x^2 + 2x - 15x - 5) \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Combine like terms**
$$\int (6x^2 - 13x - 5) \, dx$$

**Step 3 — Apply the Power Rule**
Integrate term by term:
$$= 6 \left( \frac{x^3}{3} \right) - 13 \left( \frac{x^2}{2} \right) - 5x + C$$

**Step 4 — Simplify the coefficients**
$$= 2x^3 - \frac{13}{2}x^2 - 5x + C$$

---

## Answer

$$2x^3 - \frac{13}{2}x^2 - 5x + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[2x^3 - \frac{13}{2}x^2 - 5x + C\right] = 6x^2 - \frac{13}{2}(2x) - 5 = 6x^2 - 13x - 5$$
Factoring this quadratic expression gives back the original integrand:
$$= (2x - 5)(3x + 1) \quad \checkmark$$
