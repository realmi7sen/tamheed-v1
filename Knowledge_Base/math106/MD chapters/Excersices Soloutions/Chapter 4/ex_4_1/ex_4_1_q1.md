## Problem

Evaluate the integral:
$$\int (4x + 3) \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

**Properties of the Indefinite Integral**
$$\int [cf(x) + g(x)] \, dx = c\int f(x) \, dx + \int g(x) \, dx$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Separate the integral using properties of indefinite integrals**
We can split the sum and pull out the constant multiplier:
$$\int (4x + 3) \, dx = 4\int x \, dx + \int 3 \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Apply the Power Rule**
For the first term, the power of $x$ is 1. For the second term, the antiderivative of a constant $k$ is $kx$.
$$= 4 \left( \frac{x^2}{2} \right) + 3x + C$$

**Step 3 — Simplify the expression**
$$= 2x^2 + 3x + C$$

---

## Answer

$$2x^2 + 3x + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}[2x^2 + 3x + C] = 2(2x) + 3(1) + 0 = 4x + 3 \quad \checkmark$$
