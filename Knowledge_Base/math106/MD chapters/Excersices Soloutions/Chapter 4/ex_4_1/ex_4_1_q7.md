## Problem

Evaluate the integral:
$$\int \left( 3\sqrt{u} + \frac{1}{\sqrt{u}} \right) \, du$$

---

## Rule Used

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite the integrand with fractional exponents**
Roots must be converted to rational exponents before applying the power rule:
$$\int \left( 3u^{1/2} + u^{-1/2} \right) \, du$$

**Step 2 — Apply the Power Rule**
Add 1 to each fractional exponent and divide by the new exponent:
$$= 3 \left( \frac{u^{3/2}}{3/2} \right) + \frac{u^{1/2}}{1/2} + C$$

**Step 3 — Simplify the fractions**
Dividing by a fraction is equivalent to multiplying by its reciprocal:
$$= 3 \left( \frac{2}{3} \right) u^{3/2} + 2u^{1/2} + C$$
$$= 2u^{3/2} + 2u^{1/2} + C$$

---

## Answer

$$2u^{3/2} + 2u^{1/2} + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{du}\left[2u^{3/2} + 2u^{1/2} + C\right] = 2\left(\frac{3}{2}u^{1/2}\right) + 2\left(\frac{1}{2}u^{-1/2}\right) + 0 = 3u^{1/2} + u^{-1/2} = 3\sqrt{u} + \frac{1}{\sqrt{u}} \quad \checkmark$$
