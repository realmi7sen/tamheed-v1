## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int v \sqrt{9 - v^2} \, dv$$

---

## Rule Used

**Method of Substitution**
$$\int f(g(x))g'(x) \, dx = \int f(u) \, du$$

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int u^n \, du = \frac{u^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Choose a substitution $u$ and find $du$**
Let $u$ be the expression inside the radical:
$$u = 9 - v^2$$
Differentiate both sides with respect to $v$:
$$du = -2v \, dv$$
Divide by $-2$ to match the $v \, dv$ in our integrand:
$$v \, dv = -\frac{1}{2} \, du$$

**Step 2 — Substitute $u$ and $du$ into the integral**
Rewrite the radical as a fractional exponent and replace the terms:
$$\int \sqrt{9 - v^2} (v \, dv) = \int \sqrt{u} \left(-\frac{1}{2} \, du\right) = -\frac{1}{2} \int u^{1/2} \, du$$

**Step 3 — Evaluate the integral**
Apply the power rule by adding 1 to the exponent and dividing by the new exponent:
$$= -\frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{u^{3/2}}{3/2} \right) + C = -\frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{2}{3} \right) u^{3/2} + C = -\frac{1}{3}u^{3/2} + C$$

**Step 4 — Substitute back the original expression for $v$**
Replace $u$ with $9 - v^2$:
$$= -\frac{1}{3}(9 - v^2)^{3/2} + C$$

---

## Answer

$$-\frac{1}{3}(9 - v^2)^{3/2} + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dv}\left[ -\frac{1}{3}(9 - v^2)^{3/2} + C \right] = -\frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{3}{2}(9 - v^2)^{1/2} \cdot \frac{d}{dv}[9 - v^2]$$
$$= -\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{9 - v^2} \cdot (-2v) = v\sqrt{9 - v^2} \quad \checkmark$$
