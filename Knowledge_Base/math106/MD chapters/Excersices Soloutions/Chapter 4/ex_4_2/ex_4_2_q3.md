## Problem

Evaluate the integral using the given substitution, and express the answer in terms of $x$.
$$\int x^2\sqrt{3x^3 + 7} \, dx; \quad u = 3x^3 + 7$$

---

## Rule Used

**Method of Substitution**
$$\int f(g(x))g'(x) \, dx = \int f(u) \, du$$

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int u^n \, du = \frac{u^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Find the differential $du$**
Using $u = 3x^3 + 7$, differentiate with respect to $x$:
$$du = 9x^2 \, dx \implies x^2 \, dx = \frac{1}{9} \, du$$

**Step 2 — Substitute $u$ and $du$ into the integral**
Rewrite the radical as a fractional exponent and replace the terms with $u$:
$$\int \sqrt{3x^3 + 7} (x^2 \, dx) = \int \sqrt{u} \left(\frac{1}{9} \, du\right) = \frac{1}{9} \int u^{1/2} \, du$$

**Step 3 — Evaluate the integral**
Apply the power rule by adding 1 to the exponent and dividing by the new exponent:
$$= \frac{1}{9} \left( \frac{u^{3/2}}{3/2} \right) + C = \frac{1}{9} \left( \frac{2}{3} \right) u^{3/2} + C = \frac{2}{27}u^{3/2} + C$$

**Step 4 — Substitute back the original expression for $x$**
Replace $u$ with $3x^3 + 7$:
$$= \frac{2}{27}(3x^3 + 7)^{3/2} + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{2}{27}(3x^3 + 7)^{3/2} + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{2}{27}(3x^3 + 7)^{3/2} + C \right] = \frac{2}{27} \cdot \frac{3}{2}(3x^3 + 7)^{1/2} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[3x^3 + 7]$$
$$= \frac{1}{9}(3x^3 + 7)^{1/2} \cdot (9x^2) = x^2\sqrt{3x^3 + 7} \quad \checkmark$$
