## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \sqrt{3x - 2} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Method of Substitution**
$$\int f(g(x))g'(x) \, dx = \int f(u) \, du$$

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int u^n \, du = \frac{u^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Choose a substitution $u$ and find $du$**
Let $u$ be the expression under the radical:
$$u = 3x - 2$$
Differentiate both sides:
$$du = 3 \, dx \implies dx = \frac{1}{3} \, du$$

**Step 2 — Substitute $u$ and $du$ into the integral**
$$\int \sqrt{u} \left(\frac{1}{3} \, du\right) = \frac{1}{3} \int u^{1/2} \, du$$

**Step 3 — Evaluate the integral**
Apply the power rule:
$$= \frac{1}{3} \left( \frac{u^{3/2}}{3/2} \right) + C = \frac{1}{3} \left( \frac{2}{3} \right) u^{3/2} + C = \frac{2}{9}u^{3/2} + C$$

**Step 4 — Substitute back the original expression for $x$**
Replace $u$ with $3x - 2$:
$$= \frac{2}{9}(3x - 2)^{3/2} + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{2}{9}(3x - 2)^{3/2} + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{2}{9}(3x - 2)^{3/2} + C \right] = \frac{2}{9} \cdot \frac{3}{2}(3x - 2)^{1/2} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[3x - 2]$$
$$= \frac{1}{3}\sqrt{3x - 2} \cdot (3) = \sqrt{3x - 2} \quad \checkmark$$
