## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{(\sqrt{x} + 3)^4}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Method of Substitution**
$$\int f(g(x))g'(x) \, dx = \int f(u) \, du$$

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int u^n \, du = \frac{u^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Choose a substitution $u$ and find $du$**
Let $u$ be the expression inside the parentheses. Rewrite the root as a fractional exponent first ($u = x^{1/2} + 3$):
$$u = \sqrt{x} + 3$$
Differentiate both sides with respect to $x$:
$$du = \frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2} \, dx = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} \, dx$$
Multiply by 2 to isolate the remaining part of the integrand:
$$\frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx = 2 \, du$$

**Step 2 — Substitute $u$ and $du$ into the integral**
$$\int (\sqrt{x} + 3)^4 \left( \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx \right) = \int u^4 (2 \, du) = 2 \int u^4 \, du$$

**Step 3 — Evaluate the integral**
Apply the power rule:
$$= 2 \left( \frac{u^5}{5} \right) + C = \frac{2}{5}u^5 + C$$

**Step 4 — Substitute back the original expression for $x$**
Replace $u$ with $\sqrt{x} + 3$:
$$= \frac{2}{5}(\sqrt{x} + 3)^5 + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{2}{5}(\sqrt{x} + 3)^5 + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{2}{5}(\sqrt{x} + 3)^5 + C \right] = \frac{2}{5} \cdot 5(\sqrt{x} + 3)^4 \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[\sqrt{x} + 3]$$
$$= 2(\sqrt{x} + 3)^4 \cdot \left(\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}\right) = \frac{(\sqrt{x} + 3)^4}{\sqrt{x}} \quad \checkmark$$
