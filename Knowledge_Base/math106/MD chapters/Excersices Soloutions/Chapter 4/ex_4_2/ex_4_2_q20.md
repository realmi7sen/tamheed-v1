## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int (3 - s^3)^2 s^2 \, ds$$

---

## Rule Used

**Method of Substitution**
$$\int f(g(x))g'(x) \, dx = \int f(u) \, du$$

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int u^n \, du = \frac{u^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Choose a substitution $u$ and find $du$**
Let $u$ be the expression inside the parentheses:
$$u = 3 - s^3$$
Differentiate both sides with respect to $s$:
$$du = -3s^2 \, ds$$
Divide by $-3$ to isolate the $s^2 \, ds$ that is present in the integrand:
$$s^2 \, ds = -\frac{1}{3} \, du$$

**Step 2 — Substitute $u$ and $du$ into the integral**
Group the $s^2$ with $ds$ and apply the substitution:
$$\int (3 - s^3)^2 (s^2 \, ds) = \int u^2 \left(-\frac{1}{3} \, du\right) = -\frac{1}{3} \int u^2 \, du$$

**Step 3 — Evaluate the integral**
Apply the power rule:
$$= -\frac{1}{3} \left( \frac{u^3}{3} \right) + C = -\frac{1}{9}u^3 + C$$

**Step 4 — Substitute back the original expression for $s$**
Replace $u$ with $3 - s^3$:
$$= -\frac{1}{9}(3 - s^3)^3 + C$$

---

## Answer

$$-\frac{1}{9}(3 - s^3)^3 + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{ds}\left[ -\frac{1}{9}(3 - s^3)^3 + C \right] = -\frac{1}{9} \cdot 3(3 - s^3)^2 \cdot \frac{d}{ds}[3 - s^3]$$
$$= -\frac{1}{3}(3 - s^3)^2 \cdot (-3s^2) = s^2(3 - s^3)^2 \quad \checkmark$$
