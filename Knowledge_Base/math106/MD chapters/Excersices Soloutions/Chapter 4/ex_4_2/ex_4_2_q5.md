## Problem

Evaluate the integral using the given substitution, and express the answer in terms of $x$.
$$\int \frac{(1 + \sqrt{x})^3}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx; \quad u = 1 + \sqrt{x}$$

---

## Rule Used

**Method of Substitution**
$$\int f(g(x))g'(x) \, dx = \int f(u) \, du$$

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int u^n \, du = \frac{u^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Find the differential $du$**
Write the given substitution using fractional exponents, $u = 1 + x^{1/2}$, and differentiate:
$$du = \frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2} \, dx = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} \, dx$$
Multiply by 2 to isolate the remaining part of the integrand:
$$\frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx = 2 \, du$$

**Step 2 — Substitute $u$ and $du$ into the integral**
Group the denominator with $dx$:
$$\int (1 + \sqrt{x})^3 \left( \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx \right) = \int u^3 (2 \, du) = 2 \int u^3 \, du$$

**Step 3 — Evaluate the integral**
Apply the power rule:
$$= 2 \left( \frac{u^4}{4} \right) + C = \frac{1}{2}u^4 + C$$

**Step 4 — Substitute back the original expression for $x$**
Replace $u$ with $1 + \sqrt{x}$:
$$= \frac{1}{2}(1 + \sqrt{x})^4 + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{1}{2}(1 + \sqrt{x})^4 + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{1}{2}(1 + \sqrt{x})^4 + C \right] = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 4(1 + \sqrt{x})^3 \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[1 + x^{1/2}]$$
$$= 2(1 + \sqrt{x})^3 \cdot \left(\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}\right) = \frac{(1 + \sqrt{x})^3}{\sqrt{x}} \quad \checkmark$$
