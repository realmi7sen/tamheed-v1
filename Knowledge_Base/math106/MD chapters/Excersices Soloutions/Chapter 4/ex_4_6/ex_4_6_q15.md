## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int_3^2 \frac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part II)**
$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

**Difference of Squares**
$$a^2 - b^2 = (a - b)(a + b)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Simplify the integrand and find the antiderivative $F(x)$**
Factor the numerator using the difference of squares and cancel the common term. Since the interval of integration is from $3$ to $2$, $x \neq 1$, so this cancellation is valid:
$$f(x) = \frac{(x - 1)(x + 1)}{x - 1} = x + 1$$
Find the antiderivative:
$$F(x) = \int (x + 1) \, dx = \frac{x^2}{2} + x$$

**Step 2 — Evaluate $F(b) - F(a)$**
Evaluate $F(x)$ at the upper limit $2$ and the lower limit $3$:
$$\int_3^2 (x + 1) \, dx = \left[ \frac{x^2}{2} + x \right]_3^2$$

Evaluate at $x = 2$:
$$F(2) = \frac{2^2}{2} + 2 = \frac{4}{2} + 2 = 2 + 2 = 4$$

Evaluate at $x = 3$:
$$F(3) = \frac{3^2}{2} + 3 = \frac{9}{2} + 3 = \frac{9}{2} + \frac{6}{2} = \frac{15}{2}$$

**Step 3 — Subtract the results**
$$F(2) - F(3) = 4 - \frac{15}{2} = \frac{8}{2} - \frac{15}{2} = -\frac{7}{2}$$

---

## Answer

$$-\frac{7}{2}$$

---

## Check by Differentiation (Antiderivative)

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{x^2}{2} + x \right] = \frac{2x}{2} + 1 = x + 1 = \frac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1} \quad \checkmark$$
