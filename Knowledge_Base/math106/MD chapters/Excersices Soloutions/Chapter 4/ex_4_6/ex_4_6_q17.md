## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int_1^1 (4x^2 - 5)^{100} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Definite Integral at a Single Point (Zero Width Interval)**
If $f$ is defined at $a$, then:
$$\int_a^a f(x) \, dx = 0$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Observe the limits of integration**
The upper limit and lower limit of integration are both exactly $1$. 

**Step 2 — Apply the zero width property**
Because the interval has zero width (from $x=1$ to $x=1$), the area under the curve is zero regardless of the complexity of the integrand. Therefore, no antiderivative evaluation is necessary.
$$\int_1^1 (4x^2 - 5)^{100} \, dx = 0$$

---

## Answer

$$0$$
