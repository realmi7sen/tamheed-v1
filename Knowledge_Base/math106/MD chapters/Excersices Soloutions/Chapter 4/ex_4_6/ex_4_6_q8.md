## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int_1^2 \sqrt{16x^5} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part II)**
$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite the integrand and find the antiderivative $F(x)$**
Simplify the radical and express it with a fractional exponent:
$$\sqrt{16x^5} = \sqrt{16} \cdot \sqrt{x^5} = 4x^{5/2}$$
Find the antiderivative using the power rule:
$$F(x) = \int 4x^{5/2} \, dx = 4 \left( \frac{x^{7/2}}{7/2} \right) = 4 \left( \frac{2}{7} \right) x^{7/2} = \frac{8}{7}x^{7/2}$$

**Step 2 — Evaluate $F(b) - F(a)$**
Evaluate $F(x)$ at the upper limit $2$ and the lower limit $1$:
$$\int_1^2 4x^{5/2} \, dx = \left[ \frac{8}{7}x^{7/2} \right]_1^2$$

Evaluate at $x = 2$:
$$F(2) = \frac{8}{7}(2)^{7/2} = \frac{8}{7} \left( 2^3 \cdot 2^{1/2} \right) = \frac{8}{7}(8\sqrt{2}) = \frac{64\sqrt{2}}{7}$$

Evaluate at $x = 1$:
$$F(1) = \frac{8}{7}(1)^{7/2} = \frac{8}{7}$$

**Step 3 — Subtract the results**
$$F(2) - F(1) = \frac{64\sqrt{2}}{7} - \frac{8}{7} = \frac{64\sqrt{2} - 8}{7}$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{64\sqrt{2} - 8}{7}$$

---

## Check by Differentiation (Antiderivative)

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{8}{7}x^{7/2} \right] = \frac{8}{7} \cdot \frac{7}{2} x^{5/2} = 4x^{5/2} = \sqrt{16x^5} \quad \checkmark$$
