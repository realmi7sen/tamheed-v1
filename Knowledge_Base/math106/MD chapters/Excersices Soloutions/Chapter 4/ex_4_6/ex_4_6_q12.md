## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int_1^0 s^2(\sqrt[3]{s} - \sqrt{s}) \, ds$$

---

## Rule Used

**The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part II)**
$$\int_a^b f(s) \, ds = F(b) - F(a)$$

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int s^n \, ds = \frac{s^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite the integrand and find the antiderivative $F(s)$**
First, express the roots as fractional exponents and distribute the $s^2$:
$$f(s) = s^2(s^{1/3} - s^{1/2}) = s^{2 + 1/3} - s^{2 + 1/2} = s^{7/3} - s^{5/2}$$
Now, integrate term by term using the power rule:
$$F(s) = \int (s^{7/3} - s^{5/2}) \, ds = \frac{s^{10/3}}{10/3} - \frac{s^{7/2}}{7/2} = \frac{3}{10}s^{10/3} - \frac{2}{7}s^{7/2}$$

**Step 2 — Evaluate $F(b) - F(a)$**
Evaluate $F(s)$ at the upper limit $0$ and the lower limit $1$:
$$\int_1^0 (s^{7/3} - s^{5/2}) \, ds = \left[ \frac{3}{10}s^{10/3} - \frac{2}{7}s^{7/2} \right]_1^0$$

Evaluate at $s = 0$:
$$F(0) = \frac{3}{10}(0)^{10/3} - \frac{2}{7}(0)^{7/2} = 0$$

Evaluate at $s = 1$:
$$F(1) = \frac{3}{10}(1)^{10/3} - \frac{2}{7}(1)^{7/2} = \frac{3}{10} - \frac{2}{7}$$
Find a common denominator ($70$):
$$F(1) = \frac{21}{70} - \frac{20}{70} = \frac{1}{70}$$

**Step 3 — Subtract the results**
$$F(0) - F(1) = 0 - \frac{1}{70} = -\frac{1}{70}$$

---

## Answer

$$-\frac{1}{70}$$

---

## Check by Differentiation (Antiderivative)

$$\frac{d}{ds}\left[ \frac{3}{10}s^{10/3} - \frac{2}{7}s^{7/2} \right] = \frac{3}{10} \cdot \frac{10}{3} s^{7/3} - \frac{2}{7} \cdot \frac{7}{2} s^{5/2} = s^{7/3} - s^{5/2} = s^2(\sqrt[3]{s} - \sqrt{s}) \quad \checkmark$$
