## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int_1^2 \frac{5}{x^6} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part II)**
$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite the integrand and find the antiderivative $F(x)$**
Rewrite the fraction using a negative exponent so the power rule can be applied:
$$f(x) = 5x^{-6}$$
Find the antiderivative:
$$F(x) = \int 5x^{-6} \, dx = 5 \left( \frac{x^{-5}}{-5} \right) = -x^{-5} = -\frac{1}{x^5}$$

**Step 2 — Evaluate $F(b) - F(a)$**
Evaluate $F(x)$ at the upper limit $2$ and the lower limit $1$:
$$\int_1^2 5x^{-6} \, dx = \left[ -\frac{1}{x^5} \right]_1^2$$

Evaluate at $x = 2$:
$$F(2) = -\frac{1}{2^5} = -\frac{1}{32}$$

Evaluate at $x = 1$:
$$F(1) = -\frac{1}{1^5} = -1$$

**Step 3 — Subtract the results**
$$F(2) - F(1) = -\frac{1}{32} - (-1) = -\frac{1}{32} + 1 = \frac{31}{32}$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{31}{32}$$

---

## Check by Differentiation (Antiderivative)

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ -x^{-5} \right] = -(-5)x^{-6} = 5x^{-6} = \frac{5}{x^6} \quad \checkmark$$
