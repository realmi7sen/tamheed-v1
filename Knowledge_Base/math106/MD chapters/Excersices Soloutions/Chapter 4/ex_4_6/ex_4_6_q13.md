## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int_{-1}^0 (2x + 3)^2 \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part II)**
$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Expand the integrand and find the antiderivative $F(x)$**
Expand the binomial $(a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2$:
$$f(x) = (2x + 3)^2 = 4x^2 + 12x + 9$$
Integrate term by term:
$$F(x) = \int (4x^2 + 12x + 9) \, dx = 4\left(\frac{x^3}{3}\right) + 12\left(\frac{x^2}{2}\right) + 9x = \frac{4}{3}x^3 + 6x^2 + 9x$$

**Step 2 — Evaluate $F(b) - F(a)$**
Evaluate $F(x)$ at the upper limit $0$ and the lower limit $-1$:
$$\int_{-1}^0 (4x^2 + 12x + 9) \, dx = \left[ \frac{4}{3}x^3 + 6x^2 + 9x \right]_{-1}^0$$

Evaluate at $x = 0$:
$$F(0) = \frac{4}{3}(0)^3 + 6(0)^2 + 9(0) = 0$$

Evaluate at $x = -1$:
$$F(-1) = \frac{4}{3}(-1)^3 + 6(-1)^2 + 9(-1) = -\frac{4}{3} + 6 - 9 = -\frac{4}{3} - 3$$
$$F(-1) = -\frac{4}{3} - \frac{9}{3} = -\frac{13}{3}$$

**Step 3 — Subtract the results**
$$F(0) - F(-1) = 0 - \left( -\frac{13}{3} \right) = \frac{13}{3}$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{13}{3}$$

---

## Check by Differentiation (Antiderivative)

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{4}{3}x^3 + 6x^2 + 9x \right] = \frac{4}{3}(3x^2) + 6(2x) + 9 = 4x^2 + 12x + 9 = (2x + 3)^2 \quad \checkmark$$
