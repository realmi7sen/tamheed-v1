## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int_{-3}^6 |x - 4| \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Definition of Absolute Value**
$$|x - a| = \begin{cases} -(x - a) & \text{if } x < a \\ x - a & \text{if } x \ge a \end{cases}$$

**Additive Interval Property**
$$\int_a^c f(x) \, dx = \int_a^b f(x) \, dx + \int_b^c f(x) \, dx$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite the absolute value as a piecewise function**
The expression changes sign at $x = 4$.
$$|x - 4| = \begin{cases} 4 - x & \text{for } x < 4 \\ x - 4 & \text{for } x \ge 4 \end{cases}$$

**Step 2 — Split the definite integral**
Divide the integral at $x = 4$:
$$\int_{-3}^6 |x - 4| \, dx = \int_{-3}^4 (4 - x) \, dx + \int_4^6 (x - 4) \, dx$$

**Step 3 — Find the antiderivatives and evaluate both integrals**
First integral:
$$F_1(x) = \int (4 - x) \, dx = 4x - \frac{x^2}{2}$$
$$\int_{-3}^4 (4 - x) \, dx = \left[ 4x - \frac{x^2}{2} \right]_{-3}^4 = \left( 16 - \frac{16}{2} \right) - \left( -12 - \frac{9}{2} \right) = (16 - 8) - \left(-\frac{33}{2}\right) = 8 + \frac{33}{2} = \frac{16}{2} + \frac{33}{2} = \frac{49}{2}$$

Second integral:
$$F_2(x) = \int (x - 4) \, dx = \frac{x^2}{2} - 4x$$
$$\int_4^6 (x - 4) \, dx = \left[ \frac{x^2}{2} - 4x \right]_4^6 = \left( \frac{36}{2} - 24 \right) - \left( \frac{16}{2} - 16 \right) = (18 - 24) - (8 - 16) = -6 - (-8) = 2 = \frac{4}{2}$$

**Step 4 — Add the results together**
$$\int_{-3}^6 |x - 4| \, dx = \frac{49}{2} + \frac{4}{2} = \frac{53}{2}$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{53}{2}$$

---

## Check by Differentiation (Antiderivatives)

For $x < 4$:
$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ 4x - \frac{x^2}{2} \right] = 4 - x = |x - 4| \quad \checkmark$$

For $x > 4$:
$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{x^2}{2} - 4x \right] = x - 4 = |x - 4| \quad \checkmark$$
