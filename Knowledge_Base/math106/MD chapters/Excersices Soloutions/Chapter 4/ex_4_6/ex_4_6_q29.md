## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int_1^4 \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}(\sqrt{x} + 1)^3} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part II)**
$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

**Method of Substitution**
$$\int f(g(x))g'(x) \, dx = \int f(u) \, du$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Find the indefinite integral $F(x)$ using substitution**
Let $u = \sqrt{x} + 1$. Differentiating with respect to $x$ gives:
$$du = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} \, dx \implies 2 \, du = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx$$
Substitute $u$ and $du$ into the integral:
$$\int \frac{1}{(\sqrt{x} + 1)^3} \left( \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx \right) = \int \frac{1}{u^3} (2 \, du) = 2 \int u^{-3} \, du$$
Integrate using the power rule:
$$= 2 \left( \frac{u^{-2}}{-2} \right) = -u^{-2} = -\frac{1}{u^2}$$
Substitute back $u = \sqrt{x} + 1$ to get the antiderivative in terms of $x$:
$$F(x) = -\frac{1}{(\sqrt{x} + 1)^2}$$

**Step 2 — Evaluate $F(b) - F(a)$**
Evaluate $F(x)$ at the upper limit $4$ and the lower limit $1$:
$$\int_1^4 \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}(\sqrt{x} + 1)^3} \, dx = \left[ -\frac{1}{(\sqrt{x} + 1)^2} \right]_1^4$$

Evaluate at $x = 4$:
$$F(4) = -\frac{1}{(\sqrt{4} + 1)^2} = -\frac{1}{(2 + 1)^2} = -\frac{1}{3^2} = -\frac{1}{9}$$

Evaluate at $x = 1$:
$$F(1) = -\frac{1}{(\sqrt{1} + 1)^2} = -\frac{1}{(1 + 1)^2} = -\frac{1}{2^2} = -\frac{1}{4}$$

**Step 3 — Subtract the results**
$$F(4) - F(1) = -\frac{1}{9} - \left( -\frac{1}{4} \right) = -\frac{1}{9} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{-4 + 9}{36} = \frac{5}{36}$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{5}{36}$$

---

## Check by Differentiation (Antiderivative)

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ -(\sqrt{x} + 1)^{-2} \right] = -(-2)(\sqrt{x} + 1)^{-3} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[\sqrt{x} + 1]$$
$$= 2(\sqrt{x} + 1)^{-3} \cdot \left(\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}(\sqrt{x} + 1)^3} \quad \checkmark$$
