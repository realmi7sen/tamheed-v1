## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int_4^9 \frac{t - 3}{\sqrt{t}} \, dt$$

---

## Rule Used

**The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part II)**
$$\int_a^b f(t) \, dt = F(b) - F(a)$$

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int t^n \, dt = \frac{t^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite the integrand and find the antiderivative $F(t)$**
Separate the fraction and rewrite the roots as fractional exponents:
$$\frac{t - 3}{\sqrt{t}} = \frac{t}{t^{1/2}} - \frac{3}{t^{1/2}} = t^{1/2} - 3t^{-1/2}$$
Find the antiderivative term by term:
$$F(t) = \int (t^{1/2} - 3t^{-1/2}) \, dt = \frac{t^{3/2}}{3/2} - 3 \left( \frac{t^{1/2}}{1/2} \right)$$
$$F(t) = \frac{2}{3}t^{3/2} - 6t^{1/2}$$

**Step 2 — Evaluate $F(b) - F(a)$**
Evaluate $F(t)$ at the upper limit $9$ and the lower limit $4$:
$$\int_4^9 (t^{1/2} - 3t^{-1/2}) \, dt = \left[ \frac{2}{3}t^{3/2} - 6t^{1/2} \right]_4^9$$

Evaluate at $t = 9$:
$$F(9) = \frac{2}{3}(9)^{3/2} - 6(9)^{1/2} = \frac{2}{3}(27) - 6(3) = 18 - 18 = 0$$

Evaluate at $t = 4$:
$$F(4) = \frac{2}{3}(4)^{3/2} - 6(4)^{1/2} = \frac{2}{3}(8) - 6(2) = \frac{16}{3} - 12$$
$$F(4) = \frac{16}{3} - \frac{36}{3} = -\frac{20}{3}$$

**Step 3 — Subtract the results**
$$F(9) - F(4) = 0 - \left( -\frac{20}{3} \right) = \frac{20}{3}$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{20}{3}$$

---

## Check by Differentiation (Antiderivative)

$$\frac{d}{dt}\left[ \frac{2}{3}t^{3/2} - 6t^{1/2} \right] = \frac{2}{3} \cdot \frac{3}{2}t^{1/2} - 6 \cdot \frac{1}{2}t^{-1/2} = t^{1/2} - 3t^{-1/2} = \frac{t - 3}{\sqrt{t}} \quad \checkmark$$
