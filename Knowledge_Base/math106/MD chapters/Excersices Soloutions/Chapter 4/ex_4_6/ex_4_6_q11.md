## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int_{-8}^0 (\sqrt[3]{s^2} + 2) \, ds$$

---

## Rule Used

**The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part II)**
$$\int_a^b f(s) \, ds = F(b) - F(a)$$

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int s^n \, ds = \frac{s^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite the integrand and find the antiderivative $F(s)$**
Convert the cube root into a fractional exponent:
$$f(s) = s^{2/3} + 2$$
Find the antiderivative:
$$F(s) = \int (s^{2/3} + 2) \, ds = \frac{s^{5/3}}{5/3} + 2s = \frac{3}{5}s^{5/3} + 2s$$

**Step 2 — Evaluate $F(b) - F(a)$**
Evaluate $F(s)$ at the upper limit $0$ and the lower limit $-8$:
$$\int_{-8}^0 (s^{2/3} + 2) \, ds = \left[ \frac{3}{5}s^{5/3} + 2s \right]_{-8}^0$$

Evaluate at $s = 0$:
$$F(0) = \frac{3}{5}(0)^{5/3} + 2(0) = 0$$

Evaluate at $s = -8$:
$$F(-8) = \frac{3}{5}(-8)^{5/3} + 2(-8)$$
Since $\sqrt[3]{-8} = -2$, we have $(-8)^{5/3} = (-2)^5 = -32$:
$$F(-8) = \frac{3}{5}(-32) - 16 = -\frac{96}{5} - \frac{80}{5} = -\frac{176}{5}$$

**Step 3 — Subtract the results**
$$F(0) - F(-8) = 0 - \left( -\frac{176}{5} \right) = \frac{176}{5}$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{176}{5}$$

---

## Check by Differentiation (Antiderivative)

$$\frac{d}{ds}\left[ \frac{3}{5}s^{5/3} + 2s \right] = \frac{3}{5} \cdot \frac{5}{3} s^{2/3} + 2 = s^{2/3} + 2 = \sqrt[3]{s^2} + 2 \quad \checkmark$$
