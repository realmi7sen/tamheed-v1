## Problem

Evaluate the sum.
$$\sum_{k=0}^5 k(k - 1)$$

---

## Rule Used

**Definition of Summation Notation**
$$\sum_{k=m}^n a_k = a_m + a_{m+1} + \cdots + a_n$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Expand the summation**
Substitute the index values starting from $k = 0$ up to $k = 5$ into the term $k(k - 1)$:
$$\sum_{k=0}^5 k(k - 1) = 0(0 - 1) + 1(1 - 1) + 2(2 - 1) + 3(3 - 1) + 4(4 - 1) + 5(5 - 1)$$

**Step 2 — Simplify each term**
Perform the subtractions inside the parentheses, then multiply:
$$= 0(-1) + 1(0) + 2(1) + 3(2) + 4(3) + 5(4)$$
$$= 0 + 0 + 2 + 6 + 12 + 20$$

**Step 3 — Add the terms together**
$$2 + 6 + 12 + 20 = 40$$

---

## Answer

$$40$$
