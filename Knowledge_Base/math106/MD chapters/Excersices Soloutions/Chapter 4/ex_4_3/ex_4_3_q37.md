## Problem

Find the area under the graph of $f$ from $a=0$ to $b$.
$$f(x) = x^3 + 1; \quad b = 2$$

---

## Rule Used

**Area as a Limit of a Sum**
$$A = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{k=1}^n f(x_k) \Delta x$$
where $\Delta x = \frac{b-a}{n}$ and $x_k = a + k\Delta x$.

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Set up $\Delta x$ and $x_k$**
The interval is $[0, 2]$.
The width of each subinterval is:
$$\Delta x = \frac{2 - 0}{n} = \frac{2}{n}$$
The right endpoints of the subintervals are:
$$x_k = 0 + k\left(\frac{2}{n}\right) = \frac{2k}{n}$$

**Step 2 — Evaluate $f(x_k)$**
Substitute $x_k$ into the function:
$$f(x_k) = \left(\frac{2k}{n}\right)^3 + 1 = \frac{8k^3}{n^3} + 1$$

**Step 3 — Write and simplify the Riemann sum ($R_n$)**
$$R_n = \sum_{k=1}^n f(x_k) \Delta x = \sum_{k=1}^n \left( \frac{8k^3}{n^3} + 1 \right) \left(\frac{2}{n}\right)$$
Distribute $\frac{2}{n}$:
$$R_n = \sum_{k=1}^n \left( \frac{16k^3}{n^4} + \frac{2}{n} \right)$$
Split the sum and factor out constants relative to $k$:
$$R_n = \frac{16}{n^4} \sum_{k=1}^n k^3 + \frac{2}{n} \sum_{k=1}^n 1$$

**Step 4 — Apply summation formulas**
Use $\sum k^3 = \frac{n^2(n+1)^2}{4}$ and $\sum 1 = n$:
$$R_n = \frac{16}{n^4} \left[ \frac{n^2(n+1)^2}{4} \right] + \frac{2}{n} (n)$$
Simplify the expression:
$$R_n = \frac{4(n+1)^2}{n^2} + 2 = 4\left(\frac{n+1}{n}\right)^2 + 2 = 4\left(1 + \frac{1}{n}\right)^2 + 2$$

**Step 5 — Take the limit as $n \to \infty$**
$$A = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left[ 4\left(1 + \frac{1}{n}\right)^2 + 2 \right]$$
As $n \to \infty$, the term $\frac{1}{n} \to 0$:
$$A = 4(1 + 0)^2 + 2 = 4(1) + 2 = 6$$

---

## Answer

$$6$$
