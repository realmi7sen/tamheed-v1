## Problem

Express the sum in terms of $n$.
$$\sum_{k=1}^n (3k^3 + k)$$

---

## Rule Used

**Summation Formulas**
$$\sum_{k=1}^n k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$$
$$\sum_{k=1}^n k^3 = \frac{n^2(n+1)^2}{4}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Separate the sum using linearity properties**
$$\sum_{k=1}^n (3k^3 + k) = 3\sum_{k=1}^n k^3 + \sum_{k=1}^n k$$

**Step 2 — Apply the summation formulas**
$$= 3 \left[ \frac{n^2(n+1)^2}{4} \right] + \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$$

**Step 3 — Find a common denominator and combine**
Rewrite the second term with a denominator of $4$:
$$= \frac{3n^2(n+1)^2}{4} + \frac{2n(n+1)}{4}$$

**Step 4 — Factor the numerator to simplify**
Factor out the common term $n(n+1)$ from both pieces:
$$= \frac{n(n+1)}{4} \left[ 3n(n+1) + 2 \right]$$
Expand the inside of the brackets:
$$= \frac{n(n+1)(3n^2 + 3n + 2)}{4}$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{n(n+1)(3n^2 + 3n + 2)}{4}$$
