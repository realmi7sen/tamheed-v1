## Problem

Evaluate the sum.
$$\sum_{j=1}^4 (j^2 + 1)$$

---

## Rule Used

**Definition of Summation Notation**
$$\sum_{k=1}^n a_k = a_1 + a_2 + a_3 + \cdots + a_n$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Expand the summation**
Substitute the integer values of the index $j$ from $1$ up to $4$ into the general term $(j^2 + 1)$:
$$\sum_{j=1}^4 (j^2 + 1) = (1^2 + 1) + (2^2 + 1) + (3^2 + 1) + (4^2 + 1)$$

**Step 2 — Simplify each term**
Calculate the squares and add 1 for each grouping:
$$= (1 + 1) + (4 + 1) + (9 + 1) + (16 + 1)$$
$$= 2 + 5 + 10 + 17$$

**Step 3 — Add the terms together**
$$2 + 5 + 10 + 17 = 34$$

---

## Answer

$$34$$
