## Problem

Evaluate the sum.
$$\sum_{j=1}^4 (2^j + 1)$$

---

## Rule Used

**Definition of Summation Notation**
$$\sum_{k=1}^n a_k = a_1 + a_2 + a_3 + \cdots + a_n$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Expand the summation**
Substitute the index values $j = 1, 2, 3, 4$ into the general term $(2^j + 1)$:
$$\sum_{j=1}^4 (2^j + 1) = (2^1 + 1) + (2^2 + 1) + (2^3 + 1) + (2^4 + 1)$$

**Step 2 — Simplify each term**
Calculate the powers of 2 and add 1:
$$= (2 + 1) + (4 + 1) + (8 + 1) + (16 + 1)$$
$$= 3 + 5 + 9 + 17$$

**Step 3 — Add the resulting numbers**
$$3 + 5 + 9 + 17 = 34$$

---

## Answer

$$34$$
