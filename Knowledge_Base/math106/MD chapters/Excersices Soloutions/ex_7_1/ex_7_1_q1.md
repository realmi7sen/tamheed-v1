## Problem

Evaluate $\displaystyle\int x e^{-x} \, dx$.

---

## Rule Used

**Integration by Parts (7.1):**

$$\int u \, dv = uv - \int v \, du$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Choose $dv$ and $u$.**

The most complicated part of the integrand that can be readily integrated is $e^{-x}\,dx$. Thus, we let

$$dv = e^{-x} \, dx \qquad u = x$$

$$v = -e^{-x} \qquad du = dx$$

**Step 2 — Apply the integration by parts formula.**

$$\int x e^{-x} \, dx = x(-e^{-x}) - \int (-e^{-x}) \, dx$$

$$= -x e^{-x} + \int e^{-x} \, dx$$

**Step 3 — Evaluate the remaining integral.**

$$\int e^{-x} \, dx = -e^{-x} + C$$

**Step 4 — Combine.**

$$\int x e^{-x} \, dx = -x e^{-x} - e^{-x} + C = -(x + 1)e^{-x} + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\int x e^{-x} \, dx = -(x + 1)e^{-x} + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[-(x + 1)e^{-x}\right] = -e^{-x} + (x + 1)e^{-x} = x e^{-x} \checkmark$$
