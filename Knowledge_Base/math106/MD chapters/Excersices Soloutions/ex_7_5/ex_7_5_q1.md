## Problem
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{(x + 1)^2 + 4} \, dx$.

## Rule Used
**Standard inverse tangent form:** $\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{u^2 + a^2} \, du = \frac{1}{a}\tan^{-1}\frac{u}{a} + C$, with $u = x + 1$, $a = 2$.

## Solution
**Step 1 — Substitute.** Let $u = x + 1$, $du = dx$:
$$\int \frac{1}{u^2 + 4} \, du$$

**Step 2 — Apply the standard form** with $a = 2$:
$$= \frac{1}{2}\tan^{-1}\frac{u}{2} + C$$

## Answer
$$\int \frac{1}{(x + 1)^2 + 4} \, dx = \frac{1}{2}\tan^{-1}\frac{x + 1}{2} + C$$
