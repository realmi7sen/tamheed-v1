## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = \tan^{-1}(3x - 5)$$

---

## Rule Used

**Derivative of Inverse Tangent (Chain Rule)**
If $u$ is a differentiable function of $x$, then:
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\tan^{-1} u] = \frac{1}{1 + u^2} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify $u(x)$ and find its derivative**
Let $u = 3x - 5$. Differentiating gives:
$$\frac{du}{dx} = 3$$

**Step 2 — Apply the derivative rule for inverse tangent**
$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{1 + (3x - 5)^2} \cdot 3 = \frac{3}{1 + (3x - 5)^2}$$

**Step 3 — Expand denominator (Optional)**
Expanding the binomial $(3x - 5)^2 = 9x^2 - 30x + 25$:
$$f'(x) = \frac{3}{1 + 9x^2 - 30x + 25} = \frac{3}{9x^2 - 30x + 26}$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = \frac{3}{9x^2 - 30x + 26}$$
