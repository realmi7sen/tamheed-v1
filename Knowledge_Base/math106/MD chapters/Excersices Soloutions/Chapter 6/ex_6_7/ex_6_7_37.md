## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = \ln(\arctan(x^2))$$

---

## Rule Used

**Derivative of Natural Logarithm (Chain Rule)**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\ln u] = \frac{1}{u} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$$

**Derivative of Inverse Tangent (Chain Rule)**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\arctan v] = \frac{1}{1 + v^2} \cdot \frac{dv}{dx}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Apply outer logarithm derivative rule**
Let $u = \arctan(x^2)$. Then:
$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{\arctan(x^2)} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[\arctan(x^2)]$$

**Step 2 — Differentiate $\arctan(x^2)$**
Let $v = x^2 \implies \frac{dv}{dx} = 2x$.
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\arctan(x^2)] = \frac{1}{1 + (x^2)^2} \cdot (2x) = \frac{2x}{1 + x^4}$$

**Step 3 — Combine terms**
$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{\arctan(x^2)} \cdot \frac{2x}{1 + x^4} = \frac{2x}{(1 + x^4)\arctan(x^2)}$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = \frac{2x}{(1 + x^4)\arctan(x^2)}$$
