## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{1}{x\sqrt{x - 1}} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Inverse Secant Integration Formula**
$$\int \frac{1}{u\sqrt{u^2 - a^2}} \, du = \frac{1}{a} \sec^{-1}\left(\frac{|u|}{a}\right) + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = \sqrt{x}$. Then $u^2 = x$ and $2u \, du = dx \implies du = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} \, dx$.
Identify $a = 1$.

**Step 2 — Substitute into the integral**
Note that $x = u^2$ and $\sqrt{x - 1} = \sqrt{u^2 - 1}$:
$$\int \frac{1}{u^2 \sqrt{u^2 - 1}} (2u \, du) = 2 \int \frac{u}{u^2 \sqrt{u^2 - 1}} \, du = 2 \int \frac{1}{u\sqrt{u^2 - 1}} \, du$$

**Step 3 — Integrate**
$$2 \int \frac{1}{u\sqrt{u^2 - 1}} \, du = 2 \sec^{-1}(u) + C$$

**Step 4 — Substitute back $u = \sqrt{x}$**
$$2 \sec^{-1}(\sqrt{x}) + C$$

---

## Answer

$$2 \sec^{-1}(\sqrt{x}) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ 2 \sec^{-1}(\sqrt{x}) \right] = 2 \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}\sqrt{(\sqrt{x})^2 - 1}} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[\sqrt{x}]$$
$$= \frac{2}{\sqrt{x}\sqrt{x - 1}} \cdot \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} = \frac{2}{2x\sqrt{x - 1}} = \frac{1}{x\sqrt{x - 1}} \quad \checkmark$$
