## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{e^x}{\sqrt{16 - e^{2x}}} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Inverse Sine Integration Formula**
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{a^2 - u^2}} \, du = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{u}{a}\right) + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 ‚Äî Perform substitution**
Rewrite $e^{2x}$ as $(e^x)^2$.
Let $u = e^x$. Then $du = e^x \, dx$.
Identify $a^2 = 16 \implies a = 4$.

**Step 2 ‚Äî Substitute into the integral**
$$\int \frac{e^x}{\sqrt{16 - (e^x)^2}} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{4^2 - u^2}} \, du$$

**Step 3 ‚Äî Integrate and substitute back**
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{4^2 - u^2}} \, du = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{u}{4}\right) + C = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{e^x}{4}\right) + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\sin^{-1}\left(\frac{e^x}{4}\right) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{e^x}{4}\right) \right] = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \left(\frac{e^x}{4}\right)^2}} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{e^x}{4}\right] = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\frac{16 - e^{2x}}{16}}} \cdot \frac{e^x}{4} = \frac{4}{\sqrt{16 - e^{2x}}} \cdot \frac{e^x}{4} = \frac{e^x}{\sqrt{16 - e^{2x}}} \quad \checkmark$$
