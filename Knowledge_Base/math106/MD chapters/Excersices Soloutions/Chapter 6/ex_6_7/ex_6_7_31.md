## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = \sin^{-1}\sqrt{x}$$

---

## Rule Used

**Derivative of Inverse Sine (Chain Rule)**
If $u$ is a differentiable function of $x$, then:
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\sin^{-1} u] = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - u^2}} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify $u(x)$ and find its derivative**
Let $u = \sqrt{x} = x^{1/2}$. Differentiating gives:
$$\frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}$$

**Step 2 — Apply the derivative rule for inverse sine**
Substitute $u$ and $\frac{du}{dx}$ into the derivative formula:
$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - (\sqrt{x})^2}} \cdot \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}$$
$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - x}} \cdot \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}$$

**Step 3 — Combine radicals and simplify**
$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x(1 - x)}} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x - x^2}}$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x - x^2}}$$
