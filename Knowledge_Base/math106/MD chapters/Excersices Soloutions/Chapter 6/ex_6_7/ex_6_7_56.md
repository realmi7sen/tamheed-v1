## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{\cos x}{\sqrt{9 - \sin^2 x}} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Inverse Sine Integration Formula**
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{a^2 - u^2}} \, du = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{u}{a}\right) + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = \sin x$. Differentiating gives $du = \cos x \, dx$.
Identify $a^2 = 9 \implies a = 3$.

**Step 2 — Substitute into the integral**
$$\int \frac{\cos x}{\sqrt{9 - \sin^2 x}} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{3^2 - u^2}} \, du$$

**Step 3 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{3^2 - u^2}} \, du = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{u}{3}\right) + C = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{\sin x}{3}\right) + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\sin^{-1}\left(\frac{\sin x}{3}\right) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{\sin x}{3}\right) \right] = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \left(\frac{\sin x}{3}\right)^2}} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{\sin x}{3}\right] = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\frac{9 - \sin^2 x}{9}}} \cdot \frac{\cos x}{3} = \frac{3}{\sqrt{9 - \sin^2 x}} \cdot \frac{\cos x}{3} = \frac{\cos x}{\sqrt{9 - \sin^2 x}} \quad \checkmark$$
