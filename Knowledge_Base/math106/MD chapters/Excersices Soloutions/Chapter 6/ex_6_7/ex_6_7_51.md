## Problem

Evaluate the integral:
(a) $\int \frac{1}{x^2 + 16} \, dx$
(b) $\int_0^4 \frac{1}{x^2 + 16} \, dx$

---

## Rule Used

**Inverse Tangent Integration Formula**
$$\int \frac{1}{x^2 + a^2} \, dx = \frac{1}{a} \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{a}\right) + C$$

**The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part II)**
$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

---

## Solution

### Part (a)

Identify $a^2 = 16 \implies a = 4$.
Applying the standard integration formula directly:
$$\int \frac{1}{x^2 + 16} \, dx = \frac{1}{4} \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{4}\right) + C$$

---

### Part (b)

**Step 1 — Apply FTC Part II**
$$\int_0^4 \frac{1}{x^2 + 16} \, dx = \left[ \frac{1}{4} \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{4}\right) \right]_0^4$$

**Step 2 — Evaluate at limits**
Upper limit ($x = 4$):
$$F(4) = \frac{1}{4} \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{4}{4}\right) = \frac{1}{4} \tan^{-1}(1) = \frac{1}{4} \left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right) = \frac{\pi}{16}$$

Lower limit ($x = 0$):
$$F(0) = \frac{1}{4} \tan^{-1}(0) = 0$$

**Step 3 — Subtract**
$$F(4) - F(0) = \frac{\pi}{16} - 0 = \frac{\pi}{16}$$

---

## Answer

(a) $\frac{1}{4} \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{4}\right) + C$
(b) $\frac{\pi}{16}$

---

## Check by Differentiation (Part a)

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{1}{4} \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{4}\right) \right] = \frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{1 + \left(\frac{x}{4}\right)^2} \cdot \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{16 \left(1 + \frac{x^2}{16}\right)} = \frac{1}{x^2 + 16} \quad \checkmark$$
