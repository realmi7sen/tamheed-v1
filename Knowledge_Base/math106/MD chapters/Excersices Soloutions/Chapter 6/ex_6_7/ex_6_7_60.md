## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{1}{x\sqrt{x^6 - 4}} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Inverse Secant Integration Formula**
$$\int \frac{1}{u\sqrt{u^2 - a^2}} \, du = \frac{1}{a} \sec^{-1}\left(\frac{|u|}{a}\right) + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite denominator for substitution**
Express $x^6$ as $(x^3)^2$:
$$\int \frac{1}{x\sqrt{(x^3)^2 - 4}} \, dx$$
Multiply the numerator and denominator by $x^2$ to create $x^3$ outside the radical and $x^2 \, dx$ in the numerator:
$$\int \frac{x^2}{x^3\sqrt{(x^3)^2 - 4}} \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = x^3$. Then $du = 3x^2 \, dx \implies x^2 \, dx = \frac{1}{3} du$.
Identify $a^2 = 4 \implies a = 2$.

**Step 3 — Substitute and integrate**
$$\int \frac{1}{u\sqrt{u^2 - 2^2}} \cdot \frac{1}{3} du = \frac{1}{3} \left[ \frac{1}{2} \sec^{-1}\left(\frac{|u|}{2}\right) \right] + C = \frac{1}{6} \sec^{-1}\left(\frac{|x^3|}{2}\right) + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{1}{6} \sec^{-1}\left(\frac{|x^3|}{2}\right) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{1}{6} \sec^{-1}\left(\frac{x^3}{2}\right) \right] = \frac{1}{6} \cdot \frac{1}{\left(\frac{x^3}{2}\right)\sqrt{\left(\frac{x^3}{2}\right)^2 - 1}} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{x^3}{2}\right]$$
$$= \frac{1}{6} \cdot \frac{2}{x^3 \sqrt{\frac{x^6 - 4}{4}}} \cdot \frac{3x^2}{2} = \frac{1}{6} \cdot \frac{4}{x^3 \sqrt{x^6 - 4}} \cdot \frac{3x^2}{2} = \frac{12x^2}{12x^3 \sqrt{x^6 - 4}} = \frac{1}{x\sqrt{x^6 - 4}} \quad \checkmark$$
