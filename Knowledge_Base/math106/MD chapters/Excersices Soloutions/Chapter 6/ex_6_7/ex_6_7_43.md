## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = 3^{\arcsin(x^3)}$$

---

## Rule Used

**Derivative of General Exponential Function (Chain Rule)**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[a^u] = a^u (\ln a) \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$$

**Derivative of Inverse Sine (Chain Rule)**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\arcsin v] = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - v^2}} \cdot \frac{dv}{dx}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Apply the exponential derivative formula**
Here $a = 3$ and $u = \arcsin(x^3)$:
$$f'(x) = 3^{\arcsin(x^3)} (\ln 3) \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[\arcsin(x^3)]$$

**Step 2 — Differentiate the exponent $\arcsin(x^3)$**
Let $v = x^3 \implies \frac{dv}{dx} = 3x^2$.
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\arcsin(x^3)] = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - (x^3)^2}} \cdot (3x^2) = \frac{3x^2}{\sqrt{1 - x^6}}$$

**Step 3 — Combine all terms**
$$f'(x) = 3^{\arcsin(x^3)} (\ln 3) \cdot \frac{3x^2}{\sqrt{1 - x^6}} = \frac{3x^2 (\ln 3) 3^{\arcsin(x^3)}}{\sqrt{1 - x^6}}$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = \frac{3x^2 (\ln 3) 3^{\arcsin(x^3)}}{\sqrt{1 - x^6}}$$
