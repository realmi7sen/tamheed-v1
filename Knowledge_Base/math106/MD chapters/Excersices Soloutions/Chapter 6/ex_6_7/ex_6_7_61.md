## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{e^{2x} - 25}} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Inverse Secant Integration Formula**
$$\int \frac{1}{u\sqrt{u^2 - a^2}} \, du = \frac{1}{a} \sec^{-1}\left(\frac{|u|}{a}\right) + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite the integrand for substitution**
Multiply the numerator and denominator by $e^x$:
$$\int \frac{e^x}{e^x \sqrt{(e^x)^2 - 25}} \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = e^x$. Then $du = e^x \, dx$.
Identify $a^2 = 25 \implies a = 5$.

**Step 3 — Substitute and integrate**
$$\int \frac{1}{u\sqrt{u^2 - 5^2}} \, du = \frac{1}{5} \sec^{-1}\left(\frac{u}{5}\right) + C$$
*(Since $u = e^x > 0$, absolute value bars are not needed).*

**Step 4 — Substitute back $u = e^x$**
$$\frac{1}{5} \sec^{-1}\left(\frac{e^x}{5}\right) + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{1}{5} \sec^{-1}\left(\frac{e^x}{5}\right) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{1}{5} \sec^{-1}\left(\frac{e^x}{5}\right) \right] = \frac{1}{5} \cdot \frac{1}{\left(\frac{e^x}{5}\right)\sqrt{\left(\frac{e^x}{5}\right)^2 - 1}} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{e^x}{5}\right]$$
$$= \frac{1}{5} \cdot \frac{5}{e^x \sqrt{\frac{e^{2x} - 25}{25}}} \cdot \frac{e^x}{5} = \frac{1}{5} \cdot \frac{25}{e^x \sqrt{e^{2x} - 25}} \cdot \frac{e^x}{5} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{e^{2x} - 25}} \quad \checkmark$$
