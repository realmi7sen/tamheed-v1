## Problem

Use logarithmic differentiation to find $dy/dx$.
$$y = \sqrt{(3x^2 + 2)\sqrt{6x - 7}}$$

---

## Rule Used

**Logarithmic Differentiation Steps**
1. Take the natural logarithm ($\ln$).
2. Expand using properties of logs.
3. Differentiate implicitly.
4. Solve for $y'$ and back-substitute $y$.

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite the radicals as fractional exponents**
$$y = \left[ (3x^2 + 2)(6x - 7)^{1/2} \right]^{1/2}$$
Distribute the outer $1/2$ exponent:
$$y = (3x^2 + 2)^{1/2}(6x - 7)^{1/4}$$

**Step 2 — Take the natural logarithm of both sides and expand**
$$\ln y = \ln\left[ (3x^2 + 2)^{1/2}(6x - 7)^{1/4} \right]$$
$$\ln y = \frac{1}{2}\ln(3x^2 + 2) + \frac{1}{4}\ln(6x - 7)$$

**Step 3 — Differentiate implicitly with respect to $x$**
$$\frac{1}{y} y' = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{6x}{3x^2 + 2} \right) + \frac{1}{4} \left( \frac{6}{6x - 7} \right)$$
$$\frac{1}{y} y' = \frac{3x}{3x^2 + 2} + \frac{3}{2(6x - 7)}$$

**Step 4 — Solve for $y'$ and substitute $y$**
$$y' = y \left( \frac{3x}{3x^2 + 2} + \frac{3}{2(6x - 7)} \right)$$
Substitute $y = \sqrt{(3x^2 + 2)\sqrt{6x - 7}}$:
$$y' = \sqrt{(3x^2 + 2)\sqrt{6x - 7}} \left( \frac{3x}{3x^2 + 2} + \frac{3}{2(6x - 7)} \right)$$

---

## Answer

$$y' = \sqrt{(3x^2 + 2)\sqrt{6x - 7}} \left( \frac{3x}{3x^2 + 2} + \frac{3}{2(6x - 7)} \right)$$
