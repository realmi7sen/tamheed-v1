## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = x \ln x$$

---

## Rule Used

**Product Rule for Derivatives**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[u \cdot v] = u'v + uv'$$

**Derivative of the Natural Logarithm**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\ln x] = \frac{1}{x}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify the components for the product rule**
Let $u = x$ and $v = \ln x$.

**Step 2 — Find the derivatives of the components**
$$u' = \frac{d}{dx}[x] = 1$$
$$v' = \frac{d}{dx}[\ln x] = \frac{1}{x}$$

**Step 3 — Apply the Product Rule**
Substitute the components into the product rule formula:
$$f'(x) = u'v + uv'$$
$$f'(x) = (1)(\ln x) + (x)\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)$$

**Step 4 — Simplify the expression**
$$f'(x) = \ln x + 1$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = \ln x + 1$$
