## Problem

Use implicit differentiation to find $y'$.
$$3y - x^2 + \ln(xy) = 2$$

---

## Rule Used

**Properties of Logarithms**
$$\ln(ab) = \ln a + \ln b$$

**Implicit Differentiation**
Differentiate both sides with respect to $x$, applying the chain rule to terms involving $y$ (multiplying by $y'$).

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Simplify the logarithmic term**
Using the product rule for logarithms makes differentiation much easier by avoiding the product rule inside the derivative:
$$3y - x^2 + \ln x + \ln y = 2$$

**Step 2 — Differentiate both sides with respect to $x$**
Differentiate term by term. Remember to attach $y'$ to the derivatives of $y$:
$$\frac{d}{dx}[3y] - \frac{d}{dx}[x^2] + \frac{d}{dx}[\ln x] + \frac{d}{dx}[\ln y] = \frac{d}{dx}[2]$$
$$3y' - 2x + \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y}y' = 0$$

**Step 3 — Isolate the terms containing $y'$**
Keep the $y'$ terms on the left side and move everything else to the right side:
$$3y' + \frac{1}{y}y' = 2x - \frac{1}{x}$$

**Step 4 — Factor out $y'$ and solve**
Factor $y'$ on the left side:
$$y' \left( 3 + \frac{1}{y} \right) = 2x - \frac{1}{x}$$
Find a common denominator for both sides to simplify:
$$y' \left( \frac{3y + 1}{y} \right) = \frac{2x^2 - 1}{x}$$
Divide to isolate $y'$:
$$y' = \frac{\frac{2x^2 - 1}{x}}{\frac{3y + 1}{y}} = \frac{y(2x^2 - 1)}{x(3y + 1)}$$

---

## Answer

$$y' = \frac{y(2x^2 - 1)}{x(3y + 1)}$$
