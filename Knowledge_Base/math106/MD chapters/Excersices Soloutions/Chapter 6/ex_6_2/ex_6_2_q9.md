## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = \ln \sqrt{7 - 2x^3}$$

---

## Rule Used

**Properties of Logarithms**
$$\ln(a^n) = n \ln(a)$$

**Derivative of the Natural Logarithm (Chain Rule)**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\ln(u)] = \frac{1}{u} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Simplify the function using logarithm properties**
Before differentiating, it is much easier to simplify the expression by rewriting the square root as a fractional exponent and bringing it to the front:
$$f(x) = \ln((7 - 2x^3)^{1/2})$$
$$f(x) = \frac{1}{2} \ln(7 - 2x^3)$$

**Step 2 — Identify $u(x)$ and find $u'(x)$**
Let the inner function be $u(x) = 7 - 2x^3$.
$$u'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}[7 - 2x^3] = -6x^2$$

**Step 3 — Differentiate the simplified function**
Apply the chain rule, keeping the constant multiplier $\frac{1}{2}$ out front:
$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{u'(x)}{u(x)}$$
$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{-6x^2}{7 - 2x^3}$$

**Step 4 — Simplify the final expression**
Multiply the fractions and reduce:
$$f'(x) = \frac{-6x^2}{2(7 - 2x^3)} = \frac{-3x^2}{7 - 2x^3}$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = \frac{-3x^2}{7 - 2x^3}$$
