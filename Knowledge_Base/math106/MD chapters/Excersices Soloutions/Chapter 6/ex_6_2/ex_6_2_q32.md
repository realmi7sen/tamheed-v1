## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = \ln |\sin x|$$

---

## Rule Used

**Derivative of the Natural Logarithm of an Absolute Value**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\ln|u|] = \frac{1}{u} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$$

**Trigonometric Identities**
$$\cot x = \frac{\cos x}{\sin x}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify the inner function $u(x)$**
The expression inside the absolute value is the sine function:
$$u(x) = \sin x$$

**Step 2 — Find the derivative of the inner function, $u'(x)$**
$$u'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}[\sin x] = \cos x$$

**Step 3 — Apply the derivative rule for logarithms**
Substitute $u$ and $u'$ into the formula $f'(x) = \frac{u'(x)}{u(x)}$:
$$f'(x) = \frac{\cos x}{\sin x}$$

**Step 4 — Simplify using trigonometric identities**
The ratio of cosine to sine is the cotangent function:
$$f'(x) = \cot x$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = \cot x$$
