## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = \ln(3x^2 - 2x + 1)$$

---

## Rule Used

**Derivative of the Natural Logarithm (Chain Rule)**
If $u$ is a differentiable function of $x$, then:
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\ln(u)] = \frac{1}{u} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify the inner function $u(x)$**
The argument of the natural logarithm is our inner function:
$$u(x) = 3x^2 - 2x + 1$$

**Step 2 — Find the derivative of the inner function, $u'(x)$**
Using the power rule:
$$u'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}[3x^2 - 2x + 1] = 6x - 2$$

**Step 3 — Apply the derivative rule for logarithms**
Substitute $u$ and $u'$ into the formula $f'(x) = \frac{u'(x)}{u(x)}$:
$$f'(x) = \frac{6x - 2}{3x^2 - 2x + 1}$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = \frac{6x - 2}{3x^2 - 2x + 1}$$
