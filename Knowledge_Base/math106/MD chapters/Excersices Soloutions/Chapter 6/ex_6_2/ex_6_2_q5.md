## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = \ln |3 - 2x|$$

---

## Rule Used

**Derivative of the Natural Logarithm of an Absolute Value**
If $u$ is a differentiable function of $x$, then:
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\ln|u|] = \frac{1}{u} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$$
*(Notice that the absolute value does not change the resulting formula for the derivative).*

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify the inner function $u(x)$**
The expression inside the absolute value is:
$$u(x) = 3 - 2x$$

**Step 2 — Find the derivative of the inner function, $u'(x)$**
$$u'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}[3 - 2x] = -2$$

**Step 3 — Apply the derivative rule**
Substitute $u$ and $u'$ into the formula $f'(x) = \frac{u'(x)}{u(x)}$:
$$f'(x) = \frac{-2}{3 - 2x}$$
*(This can also be equivalently written as $\frac{2}{2x - 3}$ by multiplying the numerator and denominator by $-1$).*

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = \frac{-2}{3 - 2x}$$
