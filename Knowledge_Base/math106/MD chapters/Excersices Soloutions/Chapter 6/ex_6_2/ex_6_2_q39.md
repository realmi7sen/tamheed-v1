## Problem

Use logarithmic differentiation to find $dy/dx$.
$$y = (5x + 2)^3(6x + 1)^2$$

---

## Rule Used

**Logarithmic Differentiation**
1. Take the natural logarithm ($\ln$) of both sides.
2. Use logarithm properties ($\ln(ab) = \ln a + \ln b$ and $\ln(a^n) = n\ln a$) to expand.
3. Differentiate implicitly with respect to $x$.
4. Multiply by $y$ to solve for $y'$ and substitute the original function for $y$.

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Take the natural logarithm of both sides and expand**
$$\ln y = \ln\left[ (5x + 2)^3(6x + 1)^2 \right]$$
Split the product into a sum:
$$\ln y = \ln\left( (5x + 2)^3 \right) + \ln\left( (6x + 1)^2 \right)$$
Bring the exponents to the front:
$$\ln y = 3\ln(5x + 2) + 2\ln(6x + 1)$$

**Step 2 — Differentiate implicitly with respect to $x$**
$$\frac{1}{y} y' = 3 \left( \frac{5}{5x + 2} \right) + 2 \left( \frac{6}{6x + 1} \right)$$
$$\frac{1}{y} y' = \frac{15}{5x + 2} + \frac{12}{6x + 1}$$

**Step 3 — Solve for $y'$ and substitute $y$**
$$y' = y \left( \frac{15}{5x + 2} + \frac{12}{6x + 1} \right)$$
Substitute $y = (5x + 2)^3(6x + 1)^2$:
$$y' = (5x + 2)^3(6x + 1)^2 \left( \frac{15}{5x + 2} + \frac{12}{6x + 1} \right)$$

**Step 4 — Simplify the expression (Optional but recommended)**
Find a common denominator inside the parentheses:
$$\frac{15(6x + 1) + 12(5x + 2)}{(5x + 2)(6x + 1)} = \frac{90x + 15 + 60x + 24}{(5x + 2)(6x + 1)} = \frac{150x + 39}{(5x + 2)(6x + 1)}$$
Multiply this back into the main expression:
$$y' = (5x + 2)^3(6x + 1)^2 \left[ \frac{150x + 39}{(5x + 2)(6x + 1)} \right]$$
Cancel out one power of $(5x + 2)$ and $(6x + 1)$ from the numerator and denominator, and factor a 3 out of $(150x + 39)$:
$$y' = 3(5x + 2)^2(6x + 1)(50x + 13)$$

---

## Answer

$$y' = 3(5x + 2)^2(6x + 1)(50x + 13)$$
