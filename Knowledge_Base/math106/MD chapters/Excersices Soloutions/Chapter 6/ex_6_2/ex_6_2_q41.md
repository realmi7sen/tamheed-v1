## Problem

Use logarithmic differentiation to find $dy/dx$.
$$y = \sqrt{4x + 7}(x - 5)^3$$

---

## Rule Used

**Logarithmic Differentiation Steps**
1. Take the natural logarithm ($\ln$).
2. Expand using properties of logs.
3. Differentiate implicitly.
4. Solve for $y'$ and back-substitute $y$.

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Take the natural logarithm of both sides and expand**
Rewrite the square root as a $1/2$ power:
$$\ln y = \ln\left[ (4x + 7)^{1/2}(x - 5)^3 \right]$$
Use log properties to expand:
$$\ln y = \frac{1}{2}\ln(4x + 7) + 3\ln(x - 5)$$

**Step 2 — Differentiate implicitly with respect to $x$**
$$\frac{1}{y} y' = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{4}{4x + 7} \right) + 3 \left( \frac{1}{x - 5} \right)$$
$$\frac{1}{y} y' = \frac{2}{4x + 7} + \frac{3}{x - 5}$$

**Step 3 — Solve for $y'$ and substitute $y$**
$$y' = y \left( \frac{2}{4x + 7} + \frac{3}{x - 5} \right)$$
Substitute $y = \sqrt{4x + 7}(x - 5)^3$:
$$y' = \sqrt{4x + 7}(x - 5)^3 \left( \frac{2}{4x + 7} + \frac{3}{x - 5} \right)$$

**Step 4 — Simplify the expression**
Find a common denominator inside the parentheses:
$$\frac{2(x - 5) + 3(4x + 7)}{(4x + 7)(x - 5)} = \frac{2x - 10 + 12x + 21}{(4x + 7)(x - 5)} = \frac{14x + 11}{(4x + 7)(x - 5)}$$
Multiply this back:
$$y' = (4x + 7)^{1/2}(x - 5)^3 \left[ \frac{14x + 11}{(4x + 7)(x - 5)} \right]$$
Reduce the powers of $(4x + 7)$ and $(x - 5)$:
$$y' = (4x + 7)^{-1/2}(x - 5)^2(14x + 11) = \frac{(x - 5)^2(14x + 11)}{\sqrt{4x + 7}}$$

---

## Answer

$$y' = \frac{(x - 5)^2(14x + 11)}{\sqrt{4x + 7}}$$
