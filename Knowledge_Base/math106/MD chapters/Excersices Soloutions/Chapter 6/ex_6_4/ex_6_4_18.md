## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{e^{\sqrt{x}}}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Exponential Integration Rule**
$$\int e^u \, du = e^u + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = \sqrt{x} = x^{1/2}$. Differentiating with respect to $x$:
$$du = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} \, dx \implies 2 \, du = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Rewrite the integral in terms of $u$**
$$\int e^{\sqrt{x}} \left( \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx \right) = \int e^u (2 \, du) = 2 \int e^u \, du$$

**Step 3 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$2 \int e^u \, du = 2e^u + C = 2e^{\sqrt{x}} + C$$

---

## Answer

$$2e^{\sqrt{x}} + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ 2e^{\sqrt{x}} \right] = 2 e^{\sqrt{x}} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[\sqrt{x}] = 2 e^{\sqrt{x}} \cdot \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} = \frac{e^{\sqrt{x}}}{\sqrt{x}} \quad \checkmark$$
