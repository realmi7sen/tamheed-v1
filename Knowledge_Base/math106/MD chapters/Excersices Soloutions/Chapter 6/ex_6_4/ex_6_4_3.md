## Problem

Evaluate the integral:
(a) $\int \frac{4x}{x^2 - 9} \, dx$
(b) $\int_1^2 \frac{4x}{x^2 - 9} \, dx$

---

## Rule Used

**Logarithmic Integration Rule**
$$\int \frac{g'(x)}{g(x)} \, dx = \ln|g(x)| + C$$

**The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part II)**
$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

---

## Solution

### Part (a)

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = x^2 - 9$. Then $du = 2x \, dx \implies 2 \, du = 4x \, dx$.
Substitute into the integral:
$$\int \frac{4x}{x^2 - 9} \, dx = \int \frac{2}{u} \, du = 2 \ln|u| + C$$

**Step 2 — Substitute back**
$$2 \ln|u| + C = 2 \ln|x^2 - 9| + C$$

---

### Part (b)

**Step 1 — Apply the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus**
$$\int_1^2 \frac{4x}{x^2 - 9} \, dx = \left[ 2 \ln|x^2 - 9| \right]_1^2$$

**Step 2 — Evaluate at the limits**
Upper limit ($x = 2$):
$$F(2) = 2 \ln|2^2 - 9| = 2 \ln|-5| = 2 \ln(5)$$

Lower limit ($x = 1$):
$$F(1) = 2 \ln|1^2 - 9| = 2 \ln|-8| = 2 \ln(8)$$

**Step 3 — Subtract and simplify using logarithm properties**
$$F(2) - F(1) = 2 \ln(5) - 2 \ln(8) = 2 \ln\left(\frac{5}{8}\right)$$

---

## Answer

(a) $2 \ln|x^2 - 9| + C$
(b) $2 \ln\left(\frac{5}{8}\right)$

---

## Check by Differentiation (Part a)

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ 2 \ln|x^2 - 9| \right] = 2 \cdot \frac{2x}{x^2 - 9} = \frac{4x}{x^2 - 9} \quad \checkmark$$
