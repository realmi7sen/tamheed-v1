## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{\ln x}{x} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int u^n \, du = \frac{u^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = \ln x$. Then $du = \frac{1}{x} \, dx$.

**Step 2 — Rewrite the integral in terms of $u$**
$$\int (\ln x) \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx = \int u \, du$$

**Step 3 — Integrate using the power rule**
$$\int u \, du = \frac{u^2}{2} + C$$

**Step 4 — Substitute back $u = \ln x$**
$$\frac{(\ln x)^2}{2} + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{(\ln x)^2}{2} + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{(\ln x)^2}{2} \right] = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 2(\ln x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[\ln x] = (\ln x) \cdot \frac{1}{x} = \frac{\ln x}{x} \quad \checkmark$$
