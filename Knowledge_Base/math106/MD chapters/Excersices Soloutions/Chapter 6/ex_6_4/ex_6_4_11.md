## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{x - 2}{x^2 - 4x + 9} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Logarithmic Integration Rule**
$$\int \frac{1}{u} \, du = \ln|u| + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = x^2 - 4x + 9$. Then $du = (2x - 4) \, dx = 2(x - 2) \, dx$.
This implies $(x - 2) \, dx = \frac{1}{2} du$.

**Step 2 — Substitute into the integral**
$$\int \frac{x - 2}{x^2 - 4x + 9} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{u} \cdot \frac{1}{2} du = \frac{1}{2} \int \frac{1}{u} du$$

**Step 3 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$\frac{1}{2} \ln|u| + C = \frac{1}{2} \ln|x^2 - 4x + 9| + C$$
*(Note: Since $x^2 - 4x + 9 = (x - 2)^2 + 5 > 0$ for all real $x$, absolute value bars can be changed to standard parentheses).*

---

## Answer

$$\frac{1}{2} \ln(x^2 - 4x + 9) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{1}{2} \ln(x^2 - 4x + 9) \right] = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{2x - 4}{x^2 - 4x + 9} = \frac{x - 2}{x^2 - 4x + 9} \quad \checkmark$$
