## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{3\sin x}{1 + 2\cos x} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Logarithmic Integration Rule**
$$\int \frac{1}{u} \, du = \ln|u| + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = 1 + 2\cos x$. Differentiating with respect to $x$:
$$du = -2\sin x \, dx \implies \sin x \, dx = -\frac{1}{2} du$$

**Step 2 — Substitute into the integral**
$$\int \frac{3\sin x}{1 + 2\cos x} \, dx = 3 \int \frac{1}{u} \left(-\frac{1}{2} du\right) = -\frac{3}{2} \int \frac{1}{u} du$$

**Step 3 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$-\frac{3}{2} \ln|u| + C = -\frac{3}{2} \ln|1 + 2\cos x| + C$$

---

## Answer

$$-\frac{3}{2} \ln|1 + 2\cos x| + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ -\frac{3}{2} \ln|1 + 2\cos x| \right] = -\frac{3}{2} \cdot \frac{-2\sin x}{1 + 2\cos x} = \frac{3\sin x}{1 + 2\cos x} \quad \checkmark$$
