## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int e^{\cos x} \sin x \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Exponential Integration Rule**
$$\int e^u \, du = e^u + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = \cos x$. Differentiating with respect to $x$:
$$du = -\sin x \, dx \implies \sin x \, dx = -du$$

**Step 2 — Rewrite the integral in terms of $u$**
$$\int e^{\cos x} \sin x \, dx = \int e^u (-du) = -\int e^u \, du$$

**Step 3 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$-\int e^u \, du = -e^u + C = -e^{\cos x} + C$$

---

## Answer

$$-e^{\cos x} + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ -e^{\cos x} \right] = -e^{\cos x} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[\cos x] = -e^{\cos x} \cdot (-\sin x) = e^{\cos x} \sin x \quad \checkmark$$
