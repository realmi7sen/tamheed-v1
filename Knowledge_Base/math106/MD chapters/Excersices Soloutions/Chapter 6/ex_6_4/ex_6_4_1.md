## Problem

Evaluate the integral:
(a) $\int \frac{1}{2x + 7} \, dx$
(b) $\int_{-2}^1 \frac{1}{2x + 7} \, dx$

---

## Rule Used

**Logarithmic Integration Rule**
$$\int \frac{1}{u} \, du = \ln|u| + C$$

**The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part II)**
$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

---

## Solution

### Part (a)

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = 2x + 7$. Then $du = 2 \, dx \implies dx = \frac{1}{2} du$.
Substitute into the integral:
$$\int \frac{1}{2x + 7} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{u} \cdot \frac{1}{2} du = \frac{1}{2} \int \frac{1}{u} du$$

**Step 2 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$\frac{1}{2} \int \frac{1}{u} du = \frac{1}{2} \ln|u| + C = \frac{1}{2} \ln|2x + 7| + C$$

---

### Part (b)

**Step 1 — Apply the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus**
Using $F(x) = \frac{1}{2} \ln|2x + 7|$:
$$\int_{-2}^1 \frac{1}{2x + 7} \, dx = \left[ \frac{1}{2} \ln|2x + 7| \right]_{-2}^1$$

**Step 2 — Evaluate at the limits**
Upper limit ($x = 1$):
$$F(1) = \frac{1}{2} \ln|2(1) + 7| = \frac{1}{2} \ln(9)$$

Lower limit ($x = -2$):
$$F(-2) = \frac{1}{2} \ln|2(-2) + 7| = \frac{1}{2} \ln(3)$$

**Step 3 — Subtract and simplify**
$$F(1) - F(-2) = \frac{1}{2} \ln(9) - \frac{1}{2} \ln(3) = \frac{1}{2} \ln\left(\frac{9}{3}\right) = \frac{1}{2} \ln(3)$$

---

## Answer

(a) $\frac{1}{2} \ln|2x + 7| + C$
(b) $\frac{1}{2} \ln(3)$

---

## Check by Differentiation (Part a)

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{1}{2} \ln|2x + 7| \right] = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{2}{2x + 7} = \frac{1}{2x + 7} \quad \checkmark$$
