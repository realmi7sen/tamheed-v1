## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{81 + 16x^2}} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Integration Formula via Inverse Hyperbolic Sine**
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{a^2 + u^2}} \, du = \sinh^{-1}\left(\frac{u}{a}\right) + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Rewrite $81 + 16x^2$ as $9^2 + (4x)^2$.
Let $u = 4x \implies du = 4 \, dx \implies dx = \frac{1}{4} du$.
Identify $a^2 = 81 \implies a = 9$.

**Step 2 — Substitute into the integral**
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{9^2 + u^2}} \cdot \frac{1}{4} du = \frac{1}{4} \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{9^2 + u^2}} \, du$$

**Step 3 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$\frac{1}{4} \sinh^{-1}\left(\frac{u}{9}\right) + C = \frac{1}{4} \sinh^{-1}\left(\frac{4x}{9}\right) + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{1}{4} \sinh^{-1}\left(\frac{4x}{9}\right) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{1}{4} \sinh^{-1}\left(\frac{4x}{9}\right) \right] = \frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{\left(\frac{4x}{9}\right)^2 + 1}} \cdot \frac{4}{9} = \frac{1}{9\sqrt{\frac{16x^2 + 81}{81}}} = \frac{1}{9 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{81 + 16x^2}}{9}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{81 + 16x^2}} \quad \checkmark$$
