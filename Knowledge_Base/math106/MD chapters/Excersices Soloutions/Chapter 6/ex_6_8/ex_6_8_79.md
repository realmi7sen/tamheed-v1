## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{1}{x\sqrt{9 - x^4}} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Integration Formula via Inverse Hyperbolic Secant**
$$\int \frac{1}{u\sqrt{a^2 - u^2}} \, du = -\frac{1}{a} \text{sech}^{-1}\left(\frac{|u|}{a}\right) + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Prepare the integrand for substitution**
Rewrite $x^4$ as $(x^2)^2$. Multiply the numerator and denominator by $x$:
$$\int \frac{x}{x^2 \sqrt{9 - (x^2)^2}} \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = x^2 \implies du = 2x \, dx \implies x \, dx = \frac{1}{2} du$.
Identify $a^2 = 9 \implies a = 3$.

**Step 3 — Substitute and integrate**
$$\int \frac{1}{u\sqrt{3^2 - u^2}} \cdot \frac{1}{2} du = \frac{1}{2} \left[ -\frac{1}{3} \text{sech}^{-1}\left(\frac{u}{3}\right) \right] + C = -\frac{1}{6} \text{sech}^{-1}\left(\frac{x^2}{3}\right) + C$$

---

## Answer

$$-\frac{1}{6} \text{sech}^{-1}\left(\frac{x^2}{3}\right) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ -\frac{1}{6} \text{sech}^{-1}\left(\frac{x^2}{3}\right) \right] = -\frac{1}{6} \cdot \left( -\frac{1}{\left(\frac{x^2}{3}\right)\sqrt{1 - \left(\frac{x^2}{3}\right)^2}} \cdot \frac{2x}{3} \right)$$
$$= \frac{1}{6} \cdot \frac{3}{x^2 \sqrt{\frac{9 - x^4}{9}}} \cdot \frac{2x}{3} = \frac{1}{6} \cdot \frac{9}{x^2 \sqrt{9 - x^4}} \cdot \frac{2x}{3} = \frac{18x}{18x^2 \sqrt{9 - x^4}} = \frac{1}{x\sqrt{9 - x^4}} \quad \checkmark$$
