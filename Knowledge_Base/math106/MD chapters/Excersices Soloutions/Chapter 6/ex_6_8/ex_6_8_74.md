## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{16x^2 - 9}} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Integration Formula via Inverse Hyperbolic Cosine**
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{u^2 - a^2}} \, du = \cosh^{-1}\left(\frac{u}{a}\right) + C \quad (u > a > 0)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Rewrite $16x^2 - 9$ as $(4x)^2 - 3^2$.
Let $u = 4x \implies du = 4 \, dx \implies dx = \frac{1}{4} du$.
Identify $a^2 = 9 \implies a = 3$.

**Step 2 — Substitute into the integral**
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{u^2 - 3^2}} \cdot \frac{1}{4} du = \frac{1}{4} \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{u^2 - 3^2}} \, du$$

**Step 3 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$\frac{1}{4} \cosh^{-1}\left(\frac{u}{3}\right) + C = \frac{1}{4} \cosh^{-1}\left(\frac{4x}{3}\right) + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{1}{4} \cosh^{-1}\left(\frac{4x}{3}\right) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{1}{4} \cosh^{-1}\left(\frac{4x}{3}\right) \right] = \frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{\left(\frac{4x}{3}\right)^2 - 1}} \cdot \frac{4}{3} = \frac{1}{3\sqrt{\frac{16x^2 - 9}{9}}} = \frac{1}{3 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{16x^2 - 9}}{3}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{16x^2 - 9}} \quad \checkmark$$
