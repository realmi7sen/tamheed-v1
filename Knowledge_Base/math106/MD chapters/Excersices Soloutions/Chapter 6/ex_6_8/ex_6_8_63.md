## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = \cosh^{-1}\sqrt{x}$$

---

## Rule Used

**Derivative of Inverse Hyperbolic Cosine (Chain Rule)**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\cosh^{-1} u] = \frac{1}{\sqrt{u^2 - 1}} \cdot \frac{du}{dx} \quad (u > 1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify $u(x)$ and find its derivative**
Let $u = \sqrt{x} = x^{1/2} \implies \frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}$.

**Step 2 — Apply the derivative rule**
$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{(\sqrt{x})^2 - 1}} \cdot \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x - 1}} \cdot \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}$$

**Step 3 — Combine radicals**
$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x(x - 1)}} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x^2 - x}}$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x^2 - x}}$$
