## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = \text{sech}^{-1}(x^2)$$

---

## Rule Used

**Derivative of Inverse Hyperbolic Secant (Chain Rule)**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\text{sech}^{-1} u] = -\frac{1}{u\sqrt{1 - u^2}} \cdot \frac{du}{dx} \quad (0 < u < 1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify $u(x)$ and find its derivative**
Let $u = x^2 \implies \frac{du}{dx} = 2x$.

**Step 2 — Apply the derivative rule**
$$f'(x) = -\frac{1}{x^2 \sqrt{1 - (x^2)^2}} \cdot (2x) = -\frac{2x}{x^2 \sqrt{1 - x^4}}$$

**Step 3 — Simplify the expression**
$$f'(x) = -\frac{2}{x\sqrt{1 - x^4}}$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = -\frac{2}{x\sqrt{1 - x^4}}$$
