## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = \tanh^{-1}(-4x)$$

---

## Rule Used

**Derivative of Inverse Hyperbolic Tangent (Chain Rule)**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\tanh^{-1} u] = \frac{1}{1 - u^2} \cdot \frac{du}{dx} \quad (|u| < 1)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify $u(x)$ and find its derivative**
Let $u = -4x \implies \frac{du}{dx} = -4$.

**Step 2 — Apply the derivative rule**
$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{1 - (-4x)^2} \cdot (-4) = \frac{-4}{1 - 16x^2}$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = \frac{-4}{1 - 16x^2} \quad \text{or} \quad \frac{4}{16x^2 - 1}$$
