## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{5 - e^{2x}}} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Integration Formula via Inverse Hyperbolic Secant**
$$\int \frac{1}{u\sqrt{a^2 - u^2}} \, du = -\frac{1}{a} \text{sech}^{-1}\left(\frac{|u|}{a}\right) + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Prepare the integrand for substitution**
Multiply the numerator and denominator by $e^x$:
$$\int \frac{e^x}{e^x \sqrt{5 - (e^x)^2}} \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = e^x \implies du = e^x \, dx$.
Identify $a^2 = 5 \implies a = \sqrt{5}$.

**Step 3 — Substitute and integrate**
$$\int \frac{1}{u\sqrt{(\sqrt{5})^2 - u^2}} \, du = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \text{sech}^{-1}\left(\frac{u}{\sqrt{5}}\right) + C$$

**Step 4 — Substitute back $u = e^x$**
$$-\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \text{sech}^{-1}\left(\frac{e^x}{\sqrt{5}}\right) + C$$

---

## Answer

$$-\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \text{sech}^{-1}\left(\frac{e^x}{\sqrt{5}}\right) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ -\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \text{sech}^{-1}\left(\frac{e^x}{\sqrt{5}}\right) \right] = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \cdot \left( -\frac{1}{\left(\frac{e^x}{\sqrt{5}}\right)\sqrt{1 - \left(\frac{e^x}{\sqrt{5}}\right)^2}} \cdot \frac{e^x}{\sqrt{5}} \right)$$
$$= \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{5}}{e^x \sqrt{\frac{5 - e^{2x}}{5}}} \cdot \frac{e^x}{\sqrt{5}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \cdot \frac{5}{e^x \sqrt{5 - e^{2x}}} \cdot \frac{e^x}{\sqrt{5}} = \frac{5}{5\sqrt{5 - e^{2x}}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5 - e^{2x}}} \quad \checkmark$$
