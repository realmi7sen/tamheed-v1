## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{\sinh \sqrt{x}}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Integration of Hyperbolic Sine**
$$\int \sinh u \, du = \cosh u + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = \sqrt{x} = x^{1/2}$. Differentiating with respect to $x$:
$$du = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} \, dx \implies \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx = 2 \, du$$

**Step 2 — Rewrite and integrate**
$$\int \sinh \sqrt{x} \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \, dx\right) = \int \sinh u (2 \, du) = 2 \int \sinh u \, du$$
$$= 2 \cosh u + C = 2 \cosh \sqrt{x} + C$$

---

## Answer

$$2 \cosh \sqrt{x} + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ 2 \cosh \sqrt{x} \right] = 2 \sinh \sqrt{x} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[\sqrt{x}] = 2 \sinh \sqrt{x} \cdot \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} = \frac{\sinh \sqrt{x}}{\sqrt{x}} \quad \checkmark$$
