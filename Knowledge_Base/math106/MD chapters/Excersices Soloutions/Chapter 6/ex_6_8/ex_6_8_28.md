## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \sinh x \, \text{sech}^2 x \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int u^n \, du = \frac{u^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

**Hyperbolic Identity & Derivative**
$$\text{sech}\, x = \frac{1}{\cosh x}, \quad \frac{d}{dx}[\cosh x] = \sinh x$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite in terms of hyperbolic sine and cosine**
$$\int \sinh x \, \text{sech}^2 x \, dx = \int \frac{\sinh x}{\cosh^2 x} \, dx = \int (\cosh x)^{-2} \sinh x \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = \cosh x$. Then $du = \sinh x \, dx$.

**Step 3 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$\int u^{-2} \, du = \frac{u^{-1}}{-1} + C = -\frac{1}{u} + C = -\frac{1}{\cosh x} + C = -\text{sech}\, x + C$$

---

## Answer

$$-\text{sech}\, x + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ -\text{sech}\, x \right] = -(-\text{sech}\, x \tanh x) = \text{sech}\, x \tanh x = \frac{1}{\cosh x} \cdot \frac{\sinh x}{\cosh x} = \sinh x \, \text{sech}^2 x \quad \checkmark$$
