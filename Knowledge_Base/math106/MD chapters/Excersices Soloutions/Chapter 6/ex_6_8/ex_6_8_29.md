## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \cosh x \, \text{csch}^2 x \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Power Rule for Integration**
$$\int u^n \, du = \frac{u^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$

**Hyperbolic Identity & Derivative**
$$\text{csch}\, x = \frac{1}{\sinh x}, \quad \frac{d}{dx}[\sinh x] = \cosh x$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite in terms of hyperbolic sine and cosine**
$$\int \cosh x \, \text{csch}^2 x \, dx = \int \frac{\cosh x}{\sinh^2 x} \, dx = \int (\sinh x)^{-2} \cosh x \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = \sinh x$. Then $du = \cosh x \, dx$.

**Step 3 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$\int u^{-2} \, du = \frac{u^{-1}}{-1} + C = -\frac{1}{u} + C = -\frac{1}{\sinh x} + C = -\text{csch}\, x + C$$

---

## Answer

$$-\text{csch}\, x + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ -\text{csch}\, x \right] = -(-\text{csch}\, x \coth x) = \text{csch}\, x \coth x = \frac{1}{\sinh x} \cdot \frac{\cosh x}{\sinh x} = \cosh x \, \text{csch}^2 x \quad \checkmark$$
