## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{1}{\text{sech}\, 7x} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Reciprocal Identity for Hyperbolic Functions**
$$\frac{1}{\text{sech}\, u} = \cosh u$$

**Integration of Hyperbolic Cosine**
$$\int \cosh u \, du = \sinh u + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite using hyperbolic identities**
Rewrite the reciprocal of hyperbolic secant:
$$\int \frac{1}{\text{sech}\, 7x} \, dx = \int \cosh(7x) \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = 7x$. Then $du = 7 \, dx \implies dx = \frac{1}{7} du$.

**Step 3 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$\int \cosh(7x) \, dx = \frac{1}{7} \int \cosh u \, du = \frac{1}{7} \sinh u + C = \frac{1}{7} \sinh(7x) + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{1}{7} \sinh(7x) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{1}{7} \sinh(7x) \right] = \frac{1}{7} \cosh(7x) \cdot 7 = \cosh(7x) = \frac{1}{\text{sech}\, 7x} \quad \checkmark$$
