## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{1}{49 - 4x^2} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Integration Formula via Inverse Hyperbolic Tangent**
$$\int \frac{1}{a^2 - u^2} \, du = \frac{1}{a} \tanh^{-1}\left(\frac{u}{a}\right) + C \quad (|u| < a)$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Rewrite $49 - 4x^2$ as $7^2 - (2x)^2$.
Let $u = 2x \implies du = 2 \, dx \implies dx = \frac{1}{2} du$.
Identify $a^2 = 49 \implies a = 7$.

**Step 2 — Substitute into the integral**
$$\int \frac{1}{7^2 - u^2} \cdot \frac{1}{2} du = \frac{1}{2} \int \frac{1}{7^2 - u^2} \, du$$

**Step 3 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$\frac{1}{2} \left[ \frac{1}{7} \tanh^{-1}\left(\frac{u}{7}\right) \right] + C = \frac{1}{14} \tanh^{-1}\left(\frac{2x}{7}\right) + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{1}{14} \tanh^{-1}\left(\frac{2x}{7}\right) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{1}{14} \tanh^{-1}\left(\frac{2x}{7}\right) \right] = \frac{1}{14} \cdot \frac{1}{1 - \left(\frac{2x}{7}\right)^2} \cdot \frac{2}{7} = \frac{1}{49 \left(1 - \frac{4x^2}{49}\right)} = \frac{1}{49 - 4x^2} \quad \checkmark$$
