## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int x^2 \cosh(x^3) \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Integration of Hyperbolic Cosine**
$$\int \cosh u \, du = \sinh u + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = x^3$. Differentiating with respect to $x$:
$$du = 3x^2 \, dx \implies x^2 \, dx = \frac{1}{3} du$$

**Step 2 — Substitute into the integral**
$$\int x^2 \cosh(x^3) \, dx = \int \cosh u \cdot \frac{1}{3} du = \frac{1}{3} \int \cosh u \, du$$

**Step 3 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$\frac{1}{3} \sinh u + C = \frac{1}{3} \sinh(x^3) + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\frac{1}{3} \sinh(x^3) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{1}{3} \sinh(x^3) \right] = \frac{1}{3} \cosh(x^3) \cdot (3x^2) = x^2 \cosh(x^3) \quad \checkmark$$
