## Problem

Find the limit, if it exists.
$$\lim_{x \to 0^+} (1 + ax)^{b/x}$$

---

## Rule Used

**L'Hôpital's Rule**
If $\lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}$ yields the indeterminate form $\left[\frac{0}{0}\right]$, then:
$$\lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify the indeterminate form**
As $x \to 0^+$, $1 + ax \to 1$ and $\frac{b}{x} \to \infty$ (assuming $a, b \neq 0$).
This yields the indeterminate form $1^\infty$.

**Step 2 — Take the natural logarithm**
Let $y = (1 + ax)^{b/x}$.
$$\ln y = \frac{b}{x} \ln(1 + ax) = \frac{b \ln(1 + ax)}{x}$$

**Step 3 — Evaluate $\lim_{x \to 0^+} \ln y$**
$$\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{b \ln(1 + ax)}{x} \quad \left[\frac{0}{0}\right]$$

Apply L'Hôpital's Rule with respect to $x$:
$$\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{\frac{d}{dx}[b \ln(1 + ax)]}{\frac{d}{dx}[x]} = \lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{\frac{ab}{1 + ax}}{1} = \frac{ab}{1 + 0} = ab$$

**Step 4 — Exponentiate to find the original limit**
Since $\lim_{x \to 0^+} \ln y = ab$:
$$\lim_{x \to 0^+} y = e^{ab}$$

---

## Answer

$$e^{ab}$$
