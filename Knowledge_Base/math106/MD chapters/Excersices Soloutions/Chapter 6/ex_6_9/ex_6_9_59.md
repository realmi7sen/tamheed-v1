## Problem

Find the limit, if it exists.
$$\lim_{x \to 0^+} (e^x - 1)^x$$

---

## Rule Used

**L'Hôpital's Rule**
If $\lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}$ yields the indeterminate form $\left[\frac{\infty}{\infty}\right]$, then:
$$\lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify the indeterminate form**
As $x \to 0^+$, $e^x - 1 \to 0$ and $x \to 0$.
This gives the indeterminate form $0^0$.

**Step 2 — Take the natural logarithm**
Let $y = (e^x - 1)^x$.
$$\ln y = x \ln(e^x - 1)$$

**Step 3 — Rewrite as a fraction and apply L'Hôpital's Rule**
$$\lim_{x \to 0^+} \ln y = \lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{\ln(e^x - 1)}{\frac{1}{x}} \quad \left[\frac{-\infty}{\infty}\right]$$

Apply L'Hôpital's Rule:
$$\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{\frac{e^x}{e^x - 1}}{-\frac{1}{x^2}} = \lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{-x^2 e^x}{e^x - 1} \quad \left[\frac{0}{0}\right]$$

Apply L'Hôpital's Rule again:
$$\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{\frac{d}{dx}[-x^2 e^x]}{\frac{d}{dx}[e^x - 1]} = \lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{-2x e^x - x^2 e^x}{e^x} = \lim_{x \to 0^+} (-2x - x^2) = 0$$

**Step 4 — Exponentiate to find the original limit**
Since $\lim_{x \to 0^+} \ln y = 0$:
$$\lim_{x \to 0^+} y = e^0 = 1$$

---

## Answer

$$1$$
