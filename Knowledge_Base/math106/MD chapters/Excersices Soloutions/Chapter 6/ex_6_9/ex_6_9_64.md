## Problem

Find the limit, if it exists.
$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \left(\frac{x^2}{x - 1} - \frac{x^2}{x + 1}\right)$$

---

## Rule Used

**L'Hôpital's Rule / Algebraic Simplification**
If a limit presents an indeterminate form of type $[\infty - \infty]$, combine the fractions over a common denominator first.

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify the indeterminate form**
As $x \to \infty$, $\frac{x^2}{x - 1} \to \infty$ and $\frac{x^2}{x + 1} \to \infty$.
This yields the indeterminate form $[\infty - \infty]$.

**Step 2 — Combine over a common denominator**
$$\frac{x^2}{x - 1} - \frac{x^2}{x + 1} = \frac{x^2(x + 1) - x^2(x - 1)}{(x - 1)(x + 1)}$$
$$= \frac{(x^3 + x^2) - (x^3 - x^2)}{x^2 - 1} = \frac{2x^2}{x^2 - 1}$$

**Step 3 — Evaluate the transformed limit**
$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{2x^2}{x^2 - 1}$$
Divide numerator and denominator by $x^2$:
$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{2}{1 - \frac{1}{x^2}} = \frac{2}{1 - 0} = 2$$

---

## Answer

$$2$$
