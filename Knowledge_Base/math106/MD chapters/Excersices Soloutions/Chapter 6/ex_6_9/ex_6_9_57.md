## Problem

Find the limit, if it exists.
$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \left(1 + \frac{1}{x}\right)^{5x}$$

---

## Rule Used

**Exponential Limit Identity**
$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \left(1 + \frac{k}{x}\right)^x = e^k$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify the indeterminate form**
As $x \to \infty$, $1 + \frac{1}{x} \to 1$ and $5x \to \infty$.
This gives the indeterminate form $1^\infty$.

**Step 2 — Use logarithmic transformation**
Let $y = \left(1 + \frac{1}{x}\right)^{5x}$.
Take the natural logarithm of both sides:
$$\ln y = \ln \left(1 + \frac{1}{x}\right)^{5x} = 5x \ln \left(1 + \frac{1}{x}\right)$$

**Step 3 — Evaluate $\lim_{x \to \infty} \ln y$**
$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \ln y = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{5 \ln\left(1 + \frac{1}{x}\right)}{\frac{1}{x}} \quad \left[\frac{0}{0}\right]$$

Apply L'Hôpital's Rule with respect to $x$:
$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{5 \cdot \frac{1}{1 + 1/x} \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{x^2}\right)}{-\frac{1}{x^2}} = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{5}{1 + \frac{1}{x}} = \frac{5}{1 + 0} = 5$$

**Step 4 — Exponentiate to find the original limit**
Since $\lim_{x \to \infty} \ln y = 5$:
$$\lim_{x \to \infty} y = e^5$$

---

## Answer

$$e^5$$
