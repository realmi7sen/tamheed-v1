## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = \log \ln x$$

---

## Rule Used

**Derivative of General Logarithm Function (Base 10)**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\log u] = \frac{1}{u \ln 10} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$$

**Derivative of Natural Logarithm**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\ln x] = \frac{1}{x}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify the inner function $u(x)$**
$$u(x) = \ln x$$

**Step 2 — Find the derivative of the inner function, $u'(x)$**
$$u'(x) = \frac{1}{x}$$

**Step 3 — Apply the derivative formula**
$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{(\ln x)\ln 10} \cdot \left(\frac{1}{x}\right)$$

**Step 4 — Simplify the expression**
$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{x (\ln x) (\ln 10)}$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{x (\ln x) (\ln 10)}$$
