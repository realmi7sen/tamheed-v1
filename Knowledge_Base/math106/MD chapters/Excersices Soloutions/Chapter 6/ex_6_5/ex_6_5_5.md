## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = \log(x^4 + 3x^2 + 1)$$

---

## Rule Used

**Derivative of General Logarithm Function (Chain Rule)**
For base $10$ (common logarithm, denoted as $\log$ without a base subscript):
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\log_a u] = \frac{1}{u \ln a} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$$
When $a = 10$, $\frac{d}{dx}[\log u] = \frac{u'}{u \ln 10}$.

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify the inner function $u(x)$ and base $a$**
$$u(x) = x^4 + 3x^2 + 1, \quad a = 10$$

**Step 2 — Differentiate $u(x)$**
$$u'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}[x^4 + 3x^2 + 1] = 4x^3 + 6x$$

**Step 3 — Apply the derivative formula**
$$f'(x) = \frac{4x^3 + 6x}{(x^4 + 3x^2 + 1)\ln 10}$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = \frac{4x^3 + 6x}{(x^4 + 3x^2 + 1)\ln 10}$$
