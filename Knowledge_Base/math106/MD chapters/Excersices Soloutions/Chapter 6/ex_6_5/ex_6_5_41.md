## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int 3^{\cos x} \sin x \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Integration of General Exponential Functions**
$$\int a^u \, du = \frac{a^u}{\ln a} + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = \cos x$. Differentiating with respect to $x$:
$$du = -\sin x \, dx \implies \sin x \, dx = -du$$

**Step 2 — Rewrite the integral in terms of $u$**
$$\int 3^{\cos x} \sin x \, dx = \int 3^u (-du) = -\int 3^u \, du$$

**Step 3 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$-\int 3^u \, du = -\frac{3^u}{\ln 3} + C = -\frac{3^{\cos x}}{\ln 3} + C$$

---

## Answer

$$-\frac{3^{\cos x}}{\ln 3} + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ -\frac{3^{\cos x}}{\ln 3} \right] = -\frac{1}{\ln 3} \cdot \left( 3^{\cos x} \ln 3 \right) \cdot (-\sin x) = 3^{\cos x} \sin x \quad \checkmark$$
