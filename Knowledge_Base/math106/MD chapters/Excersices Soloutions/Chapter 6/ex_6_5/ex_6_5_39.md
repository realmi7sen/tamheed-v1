## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{1}{x \log x} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Change of Base Formula**
$$\log x = \log_{10} x = \frac{\ln x}{\ln 10}$$

**Logarithmic Integration Rule**
$$\int \frac{1}{u} \, du = \ln|u| + C$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Change the base of the logarithm**
Rewrite $\log x$ in terms of the natural logarithm:
$$\int \frac{1}{x \left(\frac{\ln x}{\ln 10}\right)} \, dx = \ln 10 \int \frac{1}{x \ln x} \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = \ln x$. Then $du = \frac{1}{x} \, dx$.

**Step 3 — Substitute and integrate**
$$\ln 10 \int \frac{1}{u} \, du = \ln 10 \cdot \ln|u| + C$$

**Step 4 — Substitute back $u = \ln x$**
$$\ln 10 \cdot \ln|\ln x| + C$$

---

## Answer

$$(\ln 10) \ln|\ln x| + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ (\ln 10) \ln|\ln x| \right] = (\ln 10) \cdot \frac{1}{\ln x} \cdot \frac{1}{x} = \frac{\ln 10}{x \ln x} = \frac{1}{x \left(\frac{\ln x}{\ln 10}\right)} = \frac{1}{x \log x} \quad \checkmark$$
