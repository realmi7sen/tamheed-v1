## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ is the given expression.
$$f(x) = x^e + e^x$$

---

## Rule Used

**Power Rule for Constant Exponents**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[x^n] = n x^{n-1}$$

**Derivative of Natural Exponential Function**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[e^x] = e^x$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Distinguish between variable base and variable exponent**
- The term $x^e$ has a variable base $x$ and a constant exponent $e$. Use the **Power Rule**.
- The term $e^x$ has a constant base $e$ and a variable exponent $x$. Use the **Exponential Rule**.

**Step 2 — Differentiate term by term**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[x^e] = e x^{e-1}$$
$$\frac{d}{dx}[e^x] = e^x$$

**Step 3 — Combine the terms**
$$f'(x) = e x^{e-1} + e^x$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = e x^{e-1} + e^x$$
