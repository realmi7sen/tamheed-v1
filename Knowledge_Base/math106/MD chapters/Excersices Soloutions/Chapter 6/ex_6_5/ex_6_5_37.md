## Problem

Evaluate the integral.
$$\int \frac{2^x}{2^x + 1} \, dx$$

---

## Rule Used

**Logarithmic Integration Rule**
$$\int \frac{1}{u} \, du = \ln|u| + C$$

**Derivative of General Exponential Function**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[2^x] = 2^x \ln 2$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Perform substitution**
Let $u = 2^x + 1$. Differentiating with respect to $x$:
$$du = 2^x \ln 2 \, dx \implies 2^x \, dx = \frac{1}{\ln 2} du$$

**Step 2 — Substitute into the integral**
$$\int \frac{2^x}{2^x + 1} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{u} \cdot \frac{1}{\ln 2} du = \frac{1}{\ln 2} \int \frac{1}{u} du$$

**Step 3 — Integrate and substitute back**
$$\frac{1}{\ln 2} \ln|u| + C = \frac{1}{\ln 2} \ln(2^x + 1) + C$$
*(Note: Since $2^x + 1 > 0$ for all real $x$, absolute value signs are replaced by parentheses).*

---

## Answer

$$\frac{\ln(2^x + 1)}{\ln 2} + C \quad \text{or} \quad \log_2(2^x + 1) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{\ln(2^x + 1)}{\ln 2} \right] = \frac{1}{\ln 2} \cdot \frac{2^x \ln 2}{2^x + 1} = \frac{2^x}{2^x + 1} \quad \checkmark$$
