## Problem

Evaluate the integral:
(a) $\int 7^x \, dx$
(b) $\int_{-2}^1 7^x \, dx$

---

## Rule Used

**Integration of General Exponential Functions**
$$\int a^x \, dx = \frac{a^x}{\ln a} + C \quad (a > 0, a \neq 1)$$

**The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part II)**
$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

---

## Solution

### Part (a)

Apply the integration rule for general exponential functions with $a = 7$:
$$\int 7^x \, dx = \frac{7^x}{\ln 7} + C$$

---

### Part (b)

**Step 1 — Apply the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus**
Using $F(x) = \frac{7^x}{\ln 7}$:
$$\int_{-2}^1 7^x \, dx = \left[ \frac{7^x}{\ln 7} \right]_{-2}^1$$

**Step 2 — Evaluate at the limits**
Upper limit ($x = 1$):
$$F(1) = \frac{7^1}{\ln 7} = \frac{7}{\ln 7}$$

Lower limit ($x = -2$):
$$F(-2) = \frac{7^{-2}}{\ln 7} = \frac{1}{49 \ln 7}$$

**Step 3 — Subtract and simplify**
$$F(1) - F(-2) = \frac{7}{\ln 7} - \frac{1}{49 \ln 7} = \frac{1}{\ln 7} \left( 7 - \frac{1}{49} \right) = \frac{1}{\ln 7} \left( \frac{343 - 1}{49} \right) = \frac{342}{49 \ln 7}$$

---

## Answer

(a) $\frac{7^x}{\ln 7} + C$
(b) $\frac{342}{49 \ln 7}$

---

## Check by Differentiation (Part a)

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[ \frac{7^x}{\ln 7} \right] = \frac{1}{\ln 7} \cdot (7^x \ln 7) = 7^x \quad \checkmark$$
