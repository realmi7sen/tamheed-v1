## Problem

Use implicit differentiation to find $y'$.
$$e^x \cot y = x e^{2y}$$

---

## Rule Used

**Product Rule**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[uv] = u'v + uv'$$

**Trigonometric & Exponential Derivatives**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\cot y] = -\csc^2 y \cdot y'$$
$$\frac{d}{dx}[e^{2y}] = 2e^{2y} \cdot y'$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Differentiate both sides with respect to $x$**
Apply the product rule on both the left and right sides:

Left side ($u = e^x, v = \cot y$):
$$\frac{d}{dx}[e^x \cot y] = (e^x)(\cot y) + (e^x)(-\csc^2 y \cdot y') = e^x \cot y - e^x \csc^2 y \cdot y'$$

Right side ($u = x, v = e^{2y}$):
$$\frac{d}{dx}[x e^{2y}] = (1)(e^{2y}) + (x)(2e^{2y} \cdot y') = e^{2y} + 2x e^{2y} y'$$

Equate both sides:
$$e^x \cot y - e^x \csc^2 y \cdot y' = e^{2y} + 2x e^{2y} y'$$

**Step 2 — Group terms with $y'$ on one side**
Move all terms containing $y'$ to the left side and all other terms to the right side:
$$-e^x \csc^2 y \cdot y' - 2x e^{2y} y' = e^{2y} - e^x \cot y$$

**Step 3 — Factor out $y'$ and solve**
$$y'(-e^x \csc^2 y - 2x e^{2y}) = e^{2y} - e^x \cot y$$
Divide to isolate $y'$:
$$y' = \frac{e^{2y} - e^x \cot y}{-e^x \csc^2 y - 2x e^{2y}}$$
Multiply numerator and denominator by $-1$ to simplify signs:
$$y' = \frac{e^x \cot y - e^{2y}}{e^x \csc^2 y + 2x e^{2y}}$$

---

## Answer

$$y' = \frac{e^x \cot y - e^{2y}}{e^x \csc^2 y + 2x e^{2y}}$$
