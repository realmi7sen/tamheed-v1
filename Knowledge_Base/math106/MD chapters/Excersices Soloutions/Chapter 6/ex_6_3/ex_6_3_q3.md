## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ equals the given expression.
$$f(x) = e^{3x^2}$$

---

## Rule Used

**Derivative of the Natural Exponential Function (Chain Rule)**
If $u$ is a differentiable function of $x$, then:
$$\frac{d}{dx}[e^u] = e^u \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify the exponent $u(x)$**
The exponent of the exponential function is:
$$u(x) = 3x^2$$

**Step 2 — Find the derivative of the exponent, $u'(x)$**
Using the power rule:
$$u'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}[3x^2] = 6x$$

**Step 3 — Apply the derivative rule for exponential functions**
Substitute $u$ and $u'$ into the formula $f'(x) = e^{u(x)} \cdot u'(x)$:
$$f'(x) = e^{3x^2} \cdot (6x)$$

**Step 4 — Rearrange for the final answer**
$$f'(x) = 6xe^{3x^2}$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = 6xe^{3x^2}$$
