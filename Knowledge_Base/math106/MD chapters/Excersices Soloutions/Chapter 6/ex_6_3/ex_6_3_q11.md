## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ equals the given expression.
$$f(x) = \frac{e^x}{x^2 + 1}$$

---

## Rule Used

**Quotient Rule**
$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{u}{v}\right] = \frac{u'v - uv'}{v^2}$$

**Derivative of the Natural Exponential Function**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[e^x] = e^x$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Identify the components for the quotient rule**
Let the numerator be $u = e^x$ and the denominator be $v = x^2 + 1$.

**Step 2 — Find the derivatives of the components**
$$u' = \frac{d}{dx}[e^x] = e^x$$
$$v' = \frac{d}{dx}[x^2 + 1] = 2x$$

**Step 3 — Apply the Quotient Rule**
Substitute the components into the quotient rule formula:
$$f'(x) = \frac{(e^x)(x^2 + 1) - (e^x)(2x)}{(x^2 + 1)^2}$$

**Step 4 — Simplify the numerator**
Factor out the common term $e^x$ in the numerator:
$$f'(x) = \frac{e^x(x^2 + 1 - 2x)}{(x^2 + 1)^2}$$
Rearrange the polynomial terms to standard form:
$$f'(x) = \frac{e^x(x^2 - 2x + 1)}{(x^2 + 1)^2}$$
*(Note: The polynomial $x^2 - 2x + 1$ can be factored further to $(x-1)^2$, but the expanded form is standard).*

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = \frac{e^x(x^2 - 2x + 1)}{(x^2 + 1)^2}$$
