## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ equals the given expression.
$$f(x) = \frac{1}{e^x + 1}$$

---

## Rule Used

**General Power Rule (Chain Rule)**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[u^n] = n u^{n-1} \cdot u'$$

**Derivative of the Natural Exponential Function**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[e^x] = e^x$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite the function**
To avoid using the quotient rule, rewrite the function with a negative exponent:
$$f(x) = (e^x + 1)^{-1}$$

**Step 2 — Identify the inner function $u(x)$**
Let the inner function be $u(x) = e^x + 1$. Its derivative is:
$$u'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}[e^x + 1] = e^x$$

**Step 3 — Apply the General Power Rule**
Differentiate the outer function and multiply by the derivative of the inner function:
$$f'(x) = -1(e^x + 1)^{-2} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[e^x + 1]$$
$$f'(x) = -(e^x + 1)^{-2} \cdot (e^x)$$

**Step 4 — Format the final answer**
Rewrite the expression as a fraction with a positive exponent:
$$f'(x) = \frac{-e^x}{(e^x + 1)^2}$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = -\frac{e^x}{(e^x + 1)^2}$$
