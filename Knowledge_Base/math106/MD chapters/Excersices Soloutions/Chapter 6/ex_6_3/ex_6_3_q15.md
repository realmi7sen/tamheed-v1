## Problem

Find $f'(x)$ if $f(x)$ equals the given expression.
$$f(x) = e^{1/x} + \frac{1}{e^x}$$

---

## Rule Used

**Derivative of the Natural Exponential Function (Chain Rule)**
$$\frac{d}{dx}[e^u] = e^u \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$$

**Properties of Exponents**
$$\frac{1}{a^n} = a^{-n}$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Rewrite the function with negative exponents**
Prepare both terms for easier differentiation by moving expressions out of the denominator:
$$f(x) = e^{x^{-1}} + e^{-x}$$

**Step 2 — Differentiate the first term**
Let $u = x^{-1}$. Its derivative is $u' = -1x^{-2} = -\frac{1}{x^2}$.
Applying the chain rule for $e^u$:
$$\frac{d}{dx}[e^{x^{-1}}] = e^{x^{-1}} \cdot (-x^{-2}) = -\frac{e^{1/x}}{x^2}$$

**Step 3 — Differentiate the second term**
Let $v = -x$. Its derivative is $v' = -1$.
Applying the chain rule for $e^v$:
$$\frac{d}{dx}[e^{-x}] = e^{-x} \cdot (-1) = -e^{-x}$$

**Step 4 — Combine the derivatives**
Add the derivatives of the individual terms together:
$$f'(x) = -\frac{e^{1/x}}{x^2} - e^{-x}$$

---

## Answer

$$f'(x) = -\frac{e^{1/x}}{x^2} - e^{-x}$$
