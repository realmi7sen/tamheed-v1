## Problem
Determine whether $\displaystyle\int_0^{\infty} \frac{x}{1 + x^2} \, dx$ converges or diverges; if it converges, find its value.

## Rule Used
**Definition of the improper integral** + substitution $u = 1 + x^2$.

## Solution
**Step 1 — Integrate** ($u = 1 + x^2$, $x\,dx = \tfrac{1}{2}du$):
$$\int \frac{x}{1 + x^2} \, dx = \frac{1}{2}\ln(1 + x^2)$$

**Step 2 — Apply the limit.**
$$\lim_{t \to \infty}\left[\frac{1}{2}\ln(1 + x^2)\right]_0^t = \lim_{t \to \infty}\frac{1}{2}\ln(1 + t^2) = \infty$$

## Answer
The limit does not exist (grows without bound), so the integral **diverges**.
