## Problem
Determine whether $\displaystyle\int_0^{\infty} e^{-2x} \, dx$ converges or diverges; if it converges, find its value.

## Rule Used
**Definition of the improper integral with infinite upper limit.**

## Solution
**Step 1 — Integrate.**
$$\int e^{-2x} \, dx = -\frac{1}{2}e^{-2x}$$

**Step 2 — Apply the limit.**
$$\lim_{t \to \infty}\left[-\frac{1}{2}e^{-2x}\right]_0^t = \lim_{t \to \infty}\left(-\frac{1}{2}e^{-2t} + \frac{1}{2}\right) = 0 + \frac{1}{2}$$

## Answer
The integral **converges** and
$$\int_0^{\infty} e^{-2x} \, dx = \frac{1}{2}$$
