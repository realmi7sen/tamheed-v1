## Problem
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{x^2 - 4x + 8} \, dx$.

## Rule Used
**Completing the square**, then the standard inverse tangent form.

## Solution
**Step 1 — Complete the square** ($x^2 - 4x + 8$ is irreducible since $b^2 - 4ac = -16 < 0$):
$$x^2 - 4x + 8 = (x^2 - 4x + 4) + 8 - 4 = (x - 2)^2 + 4$$

**Step 2 — Substitute** $u = x - 2$, $du = dx$:
$$\int \frac{1}{u^2 + 4} \, du = \frac{1}{2}\tan^{-1}\frac{u}{2} + C$$

## Answer
$$\int \frac{1}{x^2 - 4x + 8} \, dx = \frac{1}{2}\tan^{-1}\frac{x - 2}{2} + C$$
