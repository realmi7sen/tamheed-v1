## Problem
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int x\sqrt{6 - x} \, dx$.

## Rule Used
**Miscellaneous substitution:** for an expression $\sqrt{f(x)}$, try $u = f(x)$ — here $u = 6 - x$, expressing $x$ in terms of $u$.

## Solution
**Step 1 — Substitute.** Let $u = 6 - x$, so $x = 6 - u$ and $dx = -du$:
$$\int x\sqrt{6 - x} \, dx = \int (6 - u)\sqrt{u}\,(-du) = -\int \left(6u^{1/2} - u^{3/2}\right) du$$

**Step 2 — Integrate by the power rule.**
$$= -\left(6\cdot\frac{2}{3}u^{3/2} - \frac{2}{5}u^{5/2}\right) + C = -4u^{3/2} + \frac{2}{5}u^{5/2} + C$$

**Step 3 — Return to $x$.**

## Answer
$$\int x\sqrt{6 - x} \, dx = -4(6 - x)^{3/2} + \frac{2}{5}(6 - x)^{5/2} + C$$

## Check by Differentiation
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\,\cdot\,] = 6(6-x)^{1/2} - (6-x)^{3/2} = \sqrt{6-x}\,[6 - (6 - x)] = x\sqrt{6-x} \checkmark$$
