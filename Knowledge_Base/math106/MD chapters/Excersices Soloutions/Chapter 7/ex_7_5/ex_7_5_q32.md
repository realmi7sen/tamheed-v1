## Problem
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int \frac{x^{1/3} + 1}{x^{1/3} - 1} \, dx$.

## Rule Used
**Radical substitution** $u = x^{1/3}$, then **long division** of the resulting rational integrand.

## Solution
**Step 1 — Substitute.** Let $u = x^{1/3}$, $x = u^3$, $dx = 3u^2\,du$:
$$\int \frac{u + 1}{u - 1}\cdot 3u^2 \, du = 3\int \frac{u^3 + u^2}{u - 1} \, du$$

**Step 2 — Long division.**
$$\frac{u^3 + u^2}{u - 1} = u^2 + 2u + 2 + \frac{2}{u - 1}$$

**Step 3 — Integrate.**
$$3\int \left(u^2 + 2u + 2 + \frac{2}{u - 1}\right) du = u^3 + 3u^2 + 6u + 6\ln|u - 1| + C$$

**Step 4 — Return to $x$.**

## Answer
$$\int \frac{x^{1/3} + 1}{x^{1/3} - 1} \, dx = x + 3x^{2/3} + 6x^{1/3} + 6\ln\left|x^{1/3} - 1\right| + C$$
