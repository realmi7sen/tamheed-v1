## Problem
Use Theorem (7.6) to evaluate $\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{3 + 2\cos x} \, dx$.

## Rule Used
**Theorem (7.6) — half-angle substitution** $u = \tan\frac{x}{2}$:
$$\cos x = \frac{1 - u^2}{1 + u^2}, \qquad dx = \frac{2}{1 + u^2}\,du$$

## Solution
**Step 1 — Apply Theorem (7.6).**
$$\int \frac{1}{3 + 2\cdot\dfrac{1 - u^2}{1 + u^2}}\cdot\frac{2}{1 + u^2} \, du = \int \frac{2}{3(1 + u^2) + 2(1 - u^2)} \, du = \int \frac{2}{u^2 + 5} \, du$$

**Step 2 — Standard inverse tangent form** with $a = \sqrt{5}$:
$$= \frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}\tan^{-1}\frac{u}{\sqrt{5}} + C$$

## Answer
$$\int \frac{1}{3 + 2\cos x} \, dx = \frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{\tan\frac{x}{2}}{\sqrt{5}}\right) + C$$
