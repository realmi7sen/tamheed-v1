## Problem
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int \frac{\sqrt{x}}{1 + \sqrt[3]{x}} \, dx$.

## Rule Used
**LCD substitution** (the method of the book's Example 4): with roots of index 2 and 3, let $u = x^{1/6}$ where $6 = \operatorname{lcm}(2, 3)$, then **long division**.

## Solution
**Step 1 — Substitute.** Let $u = x^{1/6}$, $x = u^6$, $dx = 6u^5\,du$; then $\sqrt{x} = u^3$, $\sqrt[3]{x} = u^2$:
$$\int \frac{u^3}{1 + u^2}\cdot 6u^5 \, du = 6\int \frac{u^8}{u^2 + 1} \, du$$

**Step 2 — Long division.**
$$\frac{u^8}{u^2 + 1} = u^6 - u^4 + u^2 - 1 + \frac{1}{u^2 + 1}$$

**Step 3 — Integrate.**
$$6\int \left(u^6 - u^4 + u^2 - 1 + \frac{1}{u^2 + 1}\right) du = \frac{6u^7}{7} - \frac{6u^5}{5} + 2u^3 - 6u + 6\tan^{-1} u + C$$

**Step 4 — Return to $x$** with $u = x^{1/6}$.

## Answer
$$\int \frac{\sqrt{x}}{1 + \sqrt[3]{x}} \, dx = \frac{6}{7}x^{7/6} - \frac{6}{5}x^{5/6} + 2x^{1/2} - 6x^{1/6} + 6\tan^{-1}\left(x^{1/6}\right) + C$$
