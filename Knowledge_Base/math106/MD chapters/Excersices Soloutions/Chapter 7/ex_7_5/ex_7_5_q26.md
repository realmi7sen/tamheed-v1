## Problem
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int_0^{25} \frac{1}{\sqrt{4 + \sqrt{x}}} \, dx$.

## Rule Used
**Two successive substitutions** with change of limits: first $u = \sqrt{x}$ to remove the inner radical, then $w = 4 + u$ for the outer one.

## Solution
**Step 1 — First substitution.** Let $u = \sqrt{x}$, $x = u^2$, $dx = 2u\,du$. Limits: $0 \to 0$, $25 \to 5$:
$$\int_0^{25} \frac{1}{\sqrt{4 + \sqrt{x}}} \, dx = \int_0^5 \frac{2u}{\sqrt{4 + u}} \, du$$

**Step 2 — Second substitution.** Let $w = 4 + u$, $u = w - 4$, $du = dw$. Limits: $0 \to 4$, $5 \to 9$:
$$\int_4^9 \frac{2(w - 4)}{\sqrt{w}} \, dw = 2\int_4^9 \left(w^{1/2} - 4w^{-1/2}\right) dw$$

**Step 3 — Integrate and evaluate.**
$$= 2\left[\frac{2}{3}w^{3/2} - 8w^{1/2}\right]_4^9 = 2\left[\left(\frac{2}{3}\cdot 27 - 8\cdot 3\right) - \left(\frac{2}{3}\cdot 8 - 8\cdot 2\right)\right]$$
$$= 2\left[(18 - 24) - \left(\frac{16}{3} - 16\right)\right] = 2\left[-6 + \frac{32}{3}\right] = 2\cdot\frac{14}{3}$$

## Answer
$$\int_0^{25} \frac{1}{\sqrt{4 + \sqrt{x}}} \, dx = \frac{28}{3}$$
