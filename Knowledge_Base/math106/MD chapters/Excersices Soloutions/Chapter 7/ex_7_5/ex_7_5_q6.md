## Problem
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{7 + 6x - x^2}} \, dx$.

## Rule Used
**Completing the square under the radical**, then the inverse sine standard form.

## Solution
**Step 1 — Complete the square.**
$$7 + 6x - x^2 = 7 - (x^2 - 6x) = 7 + 9 - (x^2 - 6x + 9) = 16 - (x - 3)^2$$

**Step 2 — Substitute** $u = x - 3$, $du = dx$, with $a = 4$:
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{16 - u^2}} \, du = \sin^{-1}\frac{u}{4} + C$$

## Answer
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{7 + 6x - x^2}} \, dx = \sin^{-1}\frac{x - 3}{4} + C$$
