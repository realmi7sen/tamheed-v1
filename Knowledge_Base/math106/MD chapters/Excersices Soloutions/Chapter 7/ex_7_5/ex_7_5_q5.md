## Problem
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{4x - x^2}} \, dx$.

## Rule Used
**Completing the square under the radical**, then the standard form $\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{a^2 - u^2}} \, du = \sin^{-1}\frac{u}{a} + C$.

## Solution
**Step 1 — Complete the square** (factor out $-1$ from the $x$ terms):
$$4x - x^2 = -(x^2 - 4x) = -(x^2 - 4x + 4) + 4 = 4 - (x - 2)^2$$

**Step 2 — Substitute** $u = x - 2$, $du = dx$, with $a = 2$:
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{4 - u^2}} \, du = \sin^{-1}\frac{u}{2} + C$$

## Answer
$$\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{4x - x^2}} \, dx = \sin^{-1}\frac{x - 2}{2} + C$$
