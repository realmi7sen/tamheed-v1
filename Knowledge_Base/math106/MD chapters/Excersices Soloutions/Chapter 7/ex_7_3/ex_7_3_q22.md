## Problem
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int \frac{3x - 5}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \, dx$.

## Rule Used
**Splitting the integrand:** the $x$-part yields to the algebraic substitution $u = 1 - x^2$; the constant part is the standard form $\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}}\,dx = \sin^{-1} x + C$.

## Solution
**Step 1 — Split.**
$$\int \frac{3x - 5}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \, dx = 3\int \frac{x}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \, dx - 5\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \, dx$$

**Step 2 — First integral** with $u = 1 - x^2$, $x\,dx = -\tfrac{1}{2}du$:
$$3\int \frac{x}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \, dx = -\frac{3}{2}\int u^{-1/2} \, du = -3\sqrt{1 - x^2}$$

**Step 3 — Second integral** (standard form):
$$-5\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \, dx = -5\sin^{-1} x$$

## Answer
$$\int \frac{3x - 5}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \, dx = -3\sqrt{1 - x^2} - 5\sin^{-1} x + C$$

## Check by Differentiation
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\,\cdot\,] = \frac{3x}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} - \frac{5}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} = \frac{3x - 5}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \checkmark$$
