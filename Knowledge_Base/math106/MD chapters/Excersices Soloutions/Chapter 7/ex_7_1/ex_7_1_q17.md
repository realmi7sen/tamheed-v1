## Problem

Evaluate $\displaystyle\int e^{-x} \sin x \, dx$.

---

## Rule Used

**Integration by Parts (7.1) applied twice**, then solving algebraically for the integral (the method of the book's Example 5).

**Warning from Section 7.1:** after choosing a trigonometric form for $dv$ in the first application, we must also choose a trigonometric form in the second — mixing choices leads to a useless identity.

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — First integration by parts.**

Let $I = \displaystyle\int e^{-x}\sin x \, dx$. Choose the trigonometric form for $dv$:

$$dv = \sin x \, dx \qquad u = e^{-x}$$

$$v = -\cos x \qquad du = -e^{-x} \, dx$$

$$I = -e^{-x}\cos x - \int (-\cos x)(-e^{-x}) \, dx = -e^{-x}\cos x - \int e^{-x}\cos x \, dx \tag{1}$$

**Step 2 — Second integration by parts (trigonometric form again).**

For $\displaystyle\int e^{-x}\cos x \, dx$:

$$dv = \cos x \, dx \qquad u = e^{-x}$$

$$v = \sin x \qquad du = -e^{-x} \, dx$$

$$\int e^{-x}\cos x \, dx = e^{-x}\sin x + \int e^{-x}\sin x \, dx = e^{-x}\sin x + I \tag{2}$$

**Step 3 — Substitute (2) into (1) and solve for $I$.**

$$I = -e^{-x}\cos x - \left(e^{-x}\sin x + I\right)$$

$$I = -e^{-x}\cos x - e^{-x}\sin x - I$$

$$2I = -e^{-x}(\sin x + \cos x)$$

$$I = -\frac{1}{2}e^{-x}(\sin x + \cos x) + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\int e^{-x}\sin x \, dx = -\frac{1}{2}e^{-x}(\sin x + \cos x) + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[-\frac{1}{2}e^{-x}(\sin x + \cos x)\right] = \frac{1}{2}e^{-x}(\sin x + \cos x) - \frac{1}{2}e^{-x}(\cos x - \sin x)$$

$$= \frac{1}{2}e^{-x}(2\sin x) = e^{-x}\sin x \checkmark$$
