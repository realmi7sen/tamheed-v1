## Problem

Evaluate $\displaystyle\int \sqrt{x} \ln x \, dx$.

---

## Rule Used

**Integration by Parts (7.1):**

$$\int u \, dv = uv - \int v \, du$$

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Choose $dv$ and $u$.**

When $\ln x$ appears in a product, we let $u = \ln x$ (its derivative simplifies, while $\sqrt{x}\,dx$ is readily integrated by the power rule):

$$dv = x^{1/2} \, dx \qquad u = \ln x$$

$$v = \tfrac{2}{3}x^{3/2} \qquad du = \frac{1}{x} \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Apply the integration by parts formula.**

$$\int \sqrt{x} \ln x \, dx = \frac{2}{3}x^{3/2}\ln x - \int \frac{2}{3}x^{3/2}\cdot\frac{1}{x} \, dx$$

$$= \frac{2}{3}x^{3/2}\ln x - \frac{2}{3}\int x^{1/2} \, dx$$

**Step 3 — Evaluate the remaining integral.**

$$\frac{2}{3}\int x^{1/2} \, dx = \frac{2}{3}\cdot\frac{2}{3}x^{3/2} = \frac{4}{9}x^{3/2}$$

**Step 4 — Combine.**

$$\int \sqrt{x} \ln x \, dx = \frac{2}{3}x^{3/2}\ln x - \frac{4}{9}x^{3/2} + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\int \sqrt{x} \ln x \, dx = \frac{2}{3}x^{3/2}\ln x - \frac{4}{9}x^{3/2} + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{2}{3}x^{3/2}\ln x - \frac{4}{9}x^{3/2}\right] = x^{1/2}\ln x + \frac{2}{3}x^{1/2} - \frac{2}{3}x^{1/2} = \sqrt{x}\ln x \checkmark$$
