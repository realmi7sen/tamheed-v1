## Problem

Evaluate $\displaystyle\int x \tan^{-1} x \, dx$.

---

## Rule Used

**Integration by Parts (7.1):**

$$\int u \, dv = uv - \int v \, du$$

with the algebraic identity $\dfrac{x^2}{1 + x^2} = 1 - \dfrac{1}{1 + x^2}$.

---

## Solution

**Step 1 — Choose $dv$ and $u$.**

Since $\tan^{-1} x$ is not readily integrated but its derivative is simple, we let

$$dv = x \, dx \qquad u = \tan^{-1} x$$

$$v = \tfrac{1}{2}x^2 \qquad du = \frac{1}{1 + x^2} \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Apply the integration by parts formula.**

$$\int x \tan^{-1} x \, dx = \frac{x^2}{2}\tan^{-1} x - \frac{1}{2}\int \frac{x^2}{1 + x^2} \, dx$$

**Step 3 — Simplify the remaining integrand.**

The integrand is an improper rational expression. Dividing:

$$\frac{x^2}{1 + x^2} = \frac{(1 + x^2) - 1}{1 + x^2} = 1 - \frac{1}{1 + x^2}$$

**Step 4 — Evaluate.**

$$\frac{1}{2}\int \left(1 - \frac{1}{1 + x^2}\right) dx = \frac{1}{2}\left(x - \tan^{-1} x\right)$$

**Step 5 — Combine.**

$$\int x \tan^{-1} x \, dx = \frac{x^2}{2}\tan^{-1} x - \frac{x}{2} + \frac{1}{2}\tan^{-1} x + C$$

$$= \frac{1}{2}(x^2 + 1)\tan^{-1} x - \frac{x}{2} + C$$

---

## Answer

$$\int x \tan^{-1} x \, dx = \frac{1}{2}(x^2 + 1)\tan^{-1} x - \frac{x}{2} + C$$

---

## Check by Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{1}{2}(x^2 + 1)\tan^{-1} x - \frac{x}{2}\right] = x\tan^{-1} x + \frac{1}{2}(x^2+1)\cdot\frac{1}{1+x^2} - \frac{1}{2}$$

$$= x\tan^{-1} x + \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{2} = x\tan^{-1} x \checkmark$$
