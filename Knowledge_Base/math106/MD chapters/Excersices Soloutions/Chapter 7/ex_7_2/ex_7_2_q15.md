## Problem
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int \sqrt{\sin x}\, \cos^3 x \, dx$.

## Rule Used
**Guidelines (7.2), case 2 (odd power of cosine)** — the guideline applies even when the power of sine is fractional: split off $\cos x$, convert, substitute $u = \sin x$.

## Solution
**Step 1 — Split off one factor of $\cos x$ and convert.**
$$\int \sin^{1/2} x\cos^3 x \, dx = \int \sin^{1/2} x(1 - \sin^2 x)\cos x \, dx$$

**Step 2 — Substitute** $u = \sin x$, $du = \cos x \, dx$:
$$= \int u^{1/2}(1 - u^2) \, du = \int (u^{1/2} - u^{5/2}) \, du = \frac{2}{3}u^{3/2} - \frac{2}{7}u^{7/2} + C$$

**Step 3 — Return to $x$.**

## Answer
$$\int \sqrt{\sin x}\,\cos^3 x \, dx = \frac{2}{3}(\sin x)^{3/2} - \frac{2}{7}(\sin x)^{7/2} + C$$

## Check by Differentiation
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\,\cdot\,] = \sin^{1/2}x\cos x - \sin^{5/2}x\cos x = \sqrt{\sin x}\,\cos x(1 - \sin^2 x) = \sqrt{\sin x}\,\cos^3 x \checkmark$$
