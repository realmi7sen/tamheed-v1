## Problem
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int \frac{6x - 11}{(x - 1)^2} \, dx$.

## Rule Used
**Partial fraction decomposition — repeated linear factor:**
$$\frac{6x - 11}{(x - 1)^2} = \frac{A}{x - 1} + \frac{B}{(x - 1)^2}$$

## Solution
**Step 1 — Clear denominators.**
$$6x - 11 = A(x - 1) + B$$

**Step 2 — Find the constants.**
- $x = 1$: $\;-5 = B$
- Coefficient of $x$: $\;6 = A$

**Step 3 — Integrate.**
$$\int \left(\frac{6}{x - 1} - \frac{5}{(x - 1)^2}\right) dx = 6\ln|x - 1| - 5\cdot\frac{(x-1)^{-1}}{-1} + C$$

## Answer
$$\int \frac{6x - 11}{(x - 1)^2} \, dx = 6\ln|x - 1| + \frac{5}{x - 1} + C$$

## Check by Differentiation
$$\frac{6}{x-1} - \frac{5}{(x-1)^2} = \frac{6(x-1) - 5}{(x-1)^2} = \frac{6x - 11}{(x-1)^2} \checkmark$$
